Canada Immigration Interview Questions and Answers for PR

By Kawal Sharma19 May 2023No Comments
Share Tweet Share Pin

Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) may ask people applying for Canadian permanent residence to attend an interview. These interviews serve as an opportunity for candidates to clarify specific details regarding their background, qualifications, and intentions.

In this Canada PR guide, we will go over some of the possible questions you will be asked in this Canada immigration interview and describe the kind of answers you should give.

Seek Expert Guidance for Canada PR interview

The Purpose of the Canada PR Interview

IRCC’s Canadian Permanent Residency forms include many in-depth sections designed to convey vital information about a candidate’s background and qualifications. However, IRCC occasionally requires additional information in a few areas. In this situation, IRCC may contact the candidate and invite them to be interviewed by one of their officers as part of the Canada PR process.

9 Practical Tips for Answering Canada PR Interview Questions

  1. Be Prepared: Familiarize yourself with common interview questions related to Canadian permanent residence. Research the program requirements, Canadian values, and the country’s social, economic, and cultural aspects.
  2. Personalize Your Answers: Tailor your responses to reflect your own experiences, qualifications, and aspirations. Use specific examples and anecdotes to illustrate your points and showcase your unique strengths.
  3. Stay Positive and Enthusiastic: Demonstrate genuine enthusiasm for choosing Canada as your destination for permanent residence. Highlight the aspects that attracted you to the country and emphasize your eagerness to contribute to its society and economy.
  4. Be Courteous: Many people are nervous when they appear for their Canada PR interview. This nervousness may lead them to interrupt the officer or answer a question rudely. For this reason, it is vital to maintain a relaxed composure and answer the Canada PR interview questions in a polite and calm manner.
  5. Be Honest: IRCC officers are typically looking for key information in certain areas when they set up an interview. This may be to determine if you truly satisfy Canada PR eligibility. If you’re certain you meet Canada PR requirements, you should be honest about your life background and qualifications. You won’t have any reason to lie to the officer, and you should always avoid doing so. Attempting to lie to an IRCC officer could lead to them rejecting your application, even if you meet the program’s eligibility requirements.
  6. Be Clear and Concise: One of the biggest Canada PR interview tips to follow is to be clear and concise when answering questions. This helps the IRCC officer obtain the information they are seeking quickly and makes their job easier. It may also present you as a more genuine and honest candidate.
  7. Showcase Your Skills and Experience: Clearly articulate how your education, professional background, and qualifications align with the Canadian job market. Emphasize transferable skills, adaptability, and potential contributions to the Canadian workforce.
  8. Show Cultural Awareness: Discuss your understanding of Canadian values, multiculturalism, and inclusivity. Mention any efforts you’ve made to learn about Canadian customs, traditions, and the way of life. Highlight your willingness to embrace and contribute to Canada’s diverse society.
  9. Emphasize Long-Term Commitment: Convey your intention to settle in Canada for the long term and become an active, engaged citizen. Discuss your plans for integration, language learning, community involvement, and the potential for Canadian citizenship in the future.

How to Obtain Canada PR in 2023?

Canada Immigration Interview Questions and Answers for PR

It’s difficult to predict what exactly IRCC officers will ask you during your interview. However, most questions fall under four categories. Therefore, you should focus on each of these during your Canada PR interview preparation. We have summarized some sample Canada PR interview questions and answers below.

Personal Questions

  • Q. Have you been to Canada before?
    A. Yes, I visited Toronto for two weeks in 2018 to attend my brother’s wedding.
  • Q. Why did you choose Canada?
    A. There is a high demand for software engineers such as myself in Vancouver, where I also intend to settle. I also have two siblings living in Vancouver, and I really look forward to reuniting with them.
  • Q. Have you ever been treated for any serious physical or mental conditions?
    A. I spent two weeks in the hospital after fracturing my leg bone in a car accident in 2013. I made a full recovery and haven’t received any medical treatment since.

Educational Questions

  • Q. Why did you take 5 years to complete a 4 year Bachelor’s degree program?
    A. I initially pursued a degree in chemical engineering at the Indian Institute of Science back in 2014. However, I realized I enjoyed software engineering more than chemical engineering after taking a few programming courses. I decided to change programs and lost a full year during the transition.
  • Q. Who paid for your education?
    A. My father financially supported my education until I completed my Bachelor’s degree program.
  • Q. Do you intend to pursue further education in Canada?
    A. I am considering completing two software certification courses at George Brown University at some point in the future. However, I would like to get at least one year of work experience in Canada before doing so.

Work Experience Questions

  • Q. What will you do if you can’t find a job in your field in Canada?
    A. I am willing to work in different fields until I can secure employment as a software engineer. For example, I used to help manage day-to-day operations at my family’s restaurant in India. So I would be open to the idea of working in food services.
  • Q. Where were you employed between February 2020 and December 2020?
    A. I was working at my family business in Bangalore. The business had lost many staff members due to the COVID-19 pandemic, so I opted to help out until my family was able to sort out their staffing issues.
  • Q. What was your annual salary while you were employed at Google India Private Ltd in Bangalore?
    A. My starting salary when I first joined in February 2021 was 1 million Rupees per year or around 16,400 Canadian Dollars. The company increased my salary to 1.5 million Rupees or around $24.600 Canadian Dollars in February 2022.

Questions About Your Family

  • Q. Do you have any close family members in Canada?
    A. I have two siblings who live in Vancouver. Their presence there is actually one of the reasons I want to settle in Vancouver.
  • Q. Do you plan to live with your siblings in Canada?
    A. I intend to live independently. However, having family in the same city would feel reassuring.
  • Q. Do you intend to get married before coming to Canada?
    A. I don’t plan to get married before coming to Canada.
