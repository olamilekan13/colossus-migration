Multiple Study Visa Refusals Canada: How Immigrants Can Achieve Success

By Kawal Sharma25 April 2023No Comments
Share Tweet Share Pin

Table of Contents

International students who intend to study in Canada must apply for and obtain a study permit from Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC). While the IRCC does approve thousands of study permit applications each year, they also reject a large portion of the applications.

It’s not uncommon for prospective international students to have their study permit application rejected multiple times. If this has happened to you, don’t give up! The truth is that it is possible to be approved after facing Canadian study visa refusal multiple times.

In this guide, we will highlight some success stories from people who underwent the same struggles and discuss some strategies to improve your chances of having your study permit application approved.

Why People Choose to Study In Canada?

Many prospective international students flock to Canada for the following reasons:

Great Colleges and Universities

Canada is home to many top-ranking Colleges and Universities. Canadian universities offer high-quality education and are recognized globally. Canadian degrees, certificates, and diplomas are considered internationally equal to equivalent degrees in the U.S. and other Commonwealth countries. This includes:

  • McGill University
  • University of Toronto
  • University of Waterloo
  • University of British Columbia
  • University of Alberta

A degree from any of these educational institutions would be incredibly valuable and help students find great jobs with high salaries.

Better Quality of Life

Canada has a relatively high standard of living. International students who study in this country can enjoy this improved quality of life. They may also be able to settle in the country after completing their studies and gaining Canadian work experience under a Post-Graduate Work Permit.

Better Opportunities Post Studies

Canada offers better opportunities for students after they complete their studies. Students may be able to stay and work in Canada after graduation.

Multicultural Environment

Canada is an open, safe, and culturally diverse society. You’ll feel at home in both our communities and classrooms.

Flexible Education Options

Canada offers flexible education options, allowing students to transfer between types and levels of education without running into roadblocks common in other parts of the world.

Long-term Return on Investment

A Canadian education can provide an excellent return on investment when you use your internationally recognized Canadian qualifications to build your career.

Inspirational Success Stories with Multiple Study Visa Refusals

Here are some inspirational success stories from people who were granted a study permit after multiple refusals.

Toor Khosa

Toor Khosa is an international student who planned to study in Canada where their family had been residing. They were hopeful that having family in Canada would boost their chances of being granted a study permit. However, IRCC rejected their initial study permit application.

Toor didn’t let this rejection discourage them from applying again, and they did so shortly after. However, IRCC also rejected their second study permit application. Following this, Toor chose to take a step back and turned to the professionals at Colossus Migration for assistance.

Elaar’s Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultants reviewed Toor’s previous applications along with IRCC’s rejection letter to determine the primary rejection reason. They then helped address the rejection cause and optimized Toor’s application for their third attempt.

This third attempt was successful, and IRCC granted Toor a study permit shortly after. Toor now resides in Canada as an international student and is grateful to be able to pursue higher education in the country.

Jasmeen Kambo

Jasmeen Kambo is another international student who has always dreamed of studying in Canada. She applied for a study permit after receiving admission to a Canadian University but was rejected. The experience left her feeling distraught until a relative recommended she reach out to Elaar Consulting for assistance.

Elaar’s professional immigration consultants helped Jasmeen understand IRCC’s list of study permit application requirements and then guided her through each step of the application process. She then reapplied using Keshav and Kawal Sharma’s assistance and was finally granted a study permit.

Jasmeen is now working her way toward a degree at one of Canada’s most prestigious Universities. Her inspirational story highlights how anyone can succeed with the right preparation and enough perseverance.

7 Practical Things to Do After Refusal of Canada Student Visa?

5 Strategies to Overcome Study Permit Refusals

IRCC officers typically look for any reason to reject a study permit applicant. This means you will need to be well-prepared when you create and submit your application.

Obtaining a study permit to study in a foreign country is an exciting opportunity, but it can also be a challenging and sometimes frustrating process. If you have experienced a study permit refusal, it can be disheartening, but there are strategies you can employ to overcome this obstacle and achieve your goal of studying abroad.

Some great strategies to help you overcome a past study permit refusal in Canada include:

1. Understand the Reasons for Your Refusal:

If you have been refused a visa multiple times, it is important to understand the reasons behind the refusal. Each refusal will come with a letter outlining the reasons for the refusal. Carefully read and understand these reasons so you can address them in your next application.

Canadian Study Permit Refusal Reasons with Solutions

 

2. Seek Professional Advice

Consider seeking professional advice from an immigration consultant who can guide you through the application process and help you understand the requirements for the visa. They can also help you prepare your application and address any issues that may have caused your previous refusals.

3. Ensure Your Application is Complete

Make sure that your application is complete and that all necessary documentation is included. Ensure that you have provided accurate and honest information in your application and that you have provided all required information and documents.

4. Provide Strong Supporting Documentation

Your supporting documentation should demonstrate that you meet the requirements for the visa and that you have a genuine intention to study in a foreign country. This can include academic transcripts, letters of acceptance from the educational institution, proof of financial support, and a strong personal statement outlining your motivation for studying in a foreign country.

A Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant consultant can review your application and inform you if you need to change anything in the forms or whether you need to include additional documents to present IRCC with a stronger case for your application’s approval.

  • 9 Ways to Prove That You Will Leave Canada at the End of Your Studies
  • Proof of Financial Support for Canadian Student Visa

5. Be Prepared for an Interview

Some visa applications may require an interview. Prepare for the interview by researching the educational institution and the country, practicing your English (if required), and preparing answers to common questions.

Is it a Good Idea to Reapply after Multiple Study Visa Refusals?

Reapplying for a study visa after multiple refusals can be a difficult decision to make. However, it is important to note that a previous visa refusal does not automatically mean that your next application will be refused.

It is important to carefully review the reasons for your previous refusals, address any issues, provide strong supporting documentation, and demonstrate strong ties to your home country. Seeking professional advice can also help you prepare a strong and comprehensive application that increases your chances of success.
