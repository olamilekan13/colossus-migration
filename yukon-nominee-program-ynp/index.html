A Complete Guide to Yukon Nominee Program (YNP)

Discover various options to immigrate to the Yukon Territory through Yukon Nominee Program YNP. A complete guide to Yukon PNP, eligibility requirements, and application process.

Yukon is one of Canadas Northernmost territories, with a population of over 43,744 as of March 31, 2022. Yukon Nominee Program (YNP) is an employer-driven immigration program. It helps the Yukon government meet the population and labour market requirements by bringing in skilled foreign and semi-skilled workers who can contribute to the provinces economy.

In this guide, we will learn about various Yukon Nominee Program streams and their requirements.

What is the Yukon Nominee Program?

Canadas provinces offer distinct provincial nominee programs to address labour market shortages in specific industries by bringing in foreign workers. Similarly, the Yukon Nominee Program (YNP) has been operating since 2002.

The applicant must obtain a valid job offer from an eligible employer in the province and meet other requirements before applying for the Yukon Nominee Program. YNP also offers a pathway for foreign business owners and entrepreneurs who wish to invest in and run businesses in the territory.

Yukon Nominee Program Streams

These are the main streams under the Yukon Nominee Program:

  • Yukon Critical Impact Worker: This stream caters to employers who wish to hire foreign workers for their Yukon-based business.
  • Yukon Skilled Worker: For skilled workers with relevant work experience in the NOC TEER 0, 1, 2 or 3 jobs.
  • Yukon Express Entry: For skilled workers having an active Express Entry profile and relevant work experience, language and education requirements.
  • Yukon Community Program: For eligible employers and skilled workers living in specific participating communities of Yukon territory.
  • Yukon Business Nominee Program: For entrepreneurs and investors willing to establish and run a business in Yukon to obtain permanent residency.

Yukon Express Entry

Applicants who are qualified for the federal Express Entry pool and wish to live and work in Yukon may apply to the Yukon Express Entry stream. In March 2015, Yukon expanded its current immigration policies to include an Express Entry stream.

YNP Express Entry stream enables the province to nominate applicants already in the Express Entry pool and possesses the required training, experience, language skills, and other qualities to aid in their successful integration into Yukon’s society and labour market.

Successful candidates are offered additional 600 points for their CRS score in the Express Entry profile. The candidates must be eligible for one of the three federal economic immigration programs to apply for the Yukon Nominee Program:

  • Federal Skilled Worker Program
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program
  • Canadian Experience Class

Minimum Requirements

  • You must fulfill the requirements of one of the three federal Express Entry programs.
  • You must complete the online Express Entry application
  • You must have enough settlement funds to support yourself and any accompanying family members in Canada
  • You must have a valid job offer for permanent, full-time work from an eligible Yukon employer
  • You must have a genuine intention to live and work in Yukon.

Application Fees: No fees

Average Processing Time: 12-14 weeks

Yukon Critical Impact Worker

The Yukon Nominee Program launched this unique stream for employers who cannot find Canadians to fill specific low-skilled occupational roles and wants to hire foreign workers instead. Qualified foreign nationals with the relevant skill set and background can apply to this program. They will receive a temporary work permit and can come to Canada. Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) process their permanent residence application while they work for their Canadian employer.

Employer Requirements

Yukon employers who wish to hire foreign workers under the Yukon Critical Impact Worker program must meet the following requirements:

  • Be a Canadian permanent resident or citizen
  • Operate a business in Yukon full-time for a minimum of one year preceding the foreign workers application (three years for non-profit organizations)
  • Possess a valid business license
  • Demonstrate they made one national recruitment effort and two local recruitment efforts for the job vacancy they are attempting to fill
  • Provide free health insurance that matches the type of coverage the nominee would receive under the Health Care Insurance Plan Act
  • Must pay for the nominees air travel cost
  • Must ensure they treat the nominee according to the Canadian Human Rights Act and Yukon Human Rights Act
  • Must participate in the nominees immigration screening if they are already present in Yukon

Application fees: No fees

Processing time: 12-14 weeks

Yukon Skilled Worker Program

Yukon Nominee Program offers the Skilled Worker stream that targets skilled foreign workers similar to the Yukon Critical Impact Worker program. It is also designed for employers who wish to hire foreign workers to fill a job vacancy they could not find Canadians for. The employer requirements for this program are identical to the Critical Impact Worker program, as mentioned above.

Minimum Requirements

  • Possess 12 months of work experience in a NOC TEER 0, 1, 2 or 3 job similar to the one they will perform in Yukon
  • Possess a high school diploma
  • Score a language proficiency score of CLB 6 or higher if their work experience is in a NOC TEER 0 or 1 jobs
  • This requirement is CLB 5 if their work experience is in a NOC TEER 2 and 3 jobs

Application fees: No application fees

Processing time: 12-14 weeks

Learn more: A Step-by-Step Guide to Yukon Skilled Worker Program

Yukon Community Program

The Yukon Community Program is a unique program designed under Yukon Nominee Program. This pilot program is scheduled to run between January 2020 and June 2023. It helps employers in specific Yukon communities address seasonal labour shortages by hiring foreign workers. Such workers can eventually apply for permanent residence.

Participating communities:

  • Whitehorse
  • Watson Lake
  • Dawson City
  • Haines Junction
  • Carmacks
  • Carcross

Yukon Community Program Employer Requirements

Employers who wish to hire foreign workers through the Yukon Community Pilot Program must meet the following requirements:

  • Own a business that has been fully operational for a least 12 months
  • Be a Canadian citizen or a Canadian permanent resident
  • The business must be in good standing with Yukons Employment Standards Branch, Corporate Affairs Branch, and Workers Compensation Health and Safety Board
  • Be willing to host or mentor the nominee and provide them with housing.
  • Be willing to arrange all the nominees initial settlement activities in Yukon.

Employee Requirements

To apply for Yukon Community Program, foreign workers must meet the exact eligibility requirements as the Yukon Critical Worker and the Yukon Skilled Worker.

Yukon Business Nominee Program

The Yukon Business Nominee program is for individuals with foreign business ownership experience who wish to set up, purchase, and operate a business in Yukon. Successful candidates will be issued a two-year work permit to come to Yukon and operate their businesses. They can then apply for permanent residence at the end of these two years.

Minimum Requirements

Prospective applicants who wish to apply under the Yukon Business Nominee program must meet the following requirements:

  • Score a minimum of 65 points on the programs points assessment grid
  • Possess a high school diploma
  • Possess at least three years of business management experience
  • Possess at least five years of work experience that relates to their business
  • Have a minimum net worth of $500,000
  • Possess $300,000 in liquid assets and be willing to invest this amount in Yukon over two years
  • Work in a role that falls under NOC 0 or A at the business once operational
