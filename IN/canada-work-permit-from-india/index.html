Canadian Work Visa Types and Requirements

The Canadian Work Permit Visa allows temporary foreign workers, foreign students, entrepreneurs, and others to work in Canada. There are two main types of Canadian work permits available for those that wish to work in Canada. These are open work permits and employer-specific work permits. These can be categorized on the basis of Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) Requirements. The official programs that operate these work permits are the Temporary Foreign Worker (TFW) program, and International Mobility Program (IMP).

Open Work Permit for Canada from India

Open work permits are work permits that allow the holder to work for any employer inside Canada. In order to obtain an open work permit from India, you must be a spouse or common-law partner of an international student or temporary foreign skilled worker. Apart from that open work permits may be issued to:

  • International students who have studied and graduated from a designated educational institution are issued as Post-Graduate work permits.
  • Applicants that have already applied for Canadian permanent residence
  • Dependent family members of applicants that have applied for permanent residence
  • Spouses or common-law partners of qualifying international students or skilled workers
  • Spouses or common-law partners of applicants in the Atlantic Immigration Pilot Program
  • Refugees, refugee claimants, protected persons or their family
  • Applicants who hold a temporary resident permit
  • Young workers employed in specific programs

Employer Specific Work Permits: Who can apply?

Employer-specific work permits allow the holder to work in Canada according to certain conditions. These could be conditions related to:

  • The employer name
  • The length of time the holder can work
  • The location where the holder can work

Employer-specific work permits may be issued to Indians who possess a valid Canadian job offer backed by a Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA), which the Government of Canada has approved.

In other cases, employer-specific work permits may be issued for Indians that possess job offers that are LMIA-exempt. This includes:

  • Jobs covered by international agreements such as GATS, CUSMA, or other non-trade agreements
  • Jobs are covered by agreements between the Federal government and provincial or territorial governments
  • Self-employed technical workers, engineers, performing and creative artists
  • Workers that have transferred as intra-company transferees who possess specialized knowledge.
  • Workers under a reciprocal agreement.
  • Jobs designated by the Minister such as academic researchers, visiting professors, and guest lecturers
  • Jobs for charity or religious work

LMIA Work Permits vs. LMIA Work-Exempt Permits

LMIA Work Permit requires Labour Market Impact Assessment done by your future employer. With LMIA, employers demonstrate to the Canadian government that efforts were made to hire a Canadian citizen or permanent resident before offering you a position.

LMIA-Exempt Work Permit – issued without requiring an LMIA. Although you must have a valid job offer or must meet unique requirements to have this work permit.

You can apply for a Canada Work Permit without LMIA from India:

  • Intra-Company Transfer
  • Caregiver Visa Canada
  • Spousal Open Work Permit

Eligibility Requirements for Canada Work Permit from India

Indian applicants must meet specific requirements to be eligible for a work permit. Some of these requirements include:

  • Proving to a Canadian immigration officer that they intend to leave Canada once the work permit expires.
  • Proving that they possess enough funds to sustain themselves and any family members accompanying them to Canada until they leave.
  • Obeying the law and possessing no criminal record.
  • Not being a danger to Canada and Canadians.
  • Being in good health and completing a medical exam.
  • Not working for employers deemed ineligible by Canadian Immigration.
  • Providing a Canadian immigration officer with additional documents if requested.

Each provincial nominee program targets specific types of individuals. This includes:

  • Skilled or Semi-skilled workers that possess a job offer
  • Individuals with past study or work experience in the province
  • Individuals that intend to work in occupations that are in demand under the PNP
  • Entrepreneurs

How to Obtain a Canadian Work Permit from India?

The application process varies significantly depending on the type of Canadian work permit you are applying for from India. For instance, international students who are studying in Canadian Universities online from Indin will be given permission to work after finishing their studies under the PGWP. LMIA Work Permit applications require a job offer from a Canadian employer.

People who are exempt from the LMIA or who are not needed to apply for a Canadian work visa should be aware of the extra documents they need to submit to ensure their entry into Canada. The two primary methods for obtaining a Canada work permit visa are an employer-specific work permit and an open work permit.

Step-By-Step Canada Work Permit from India

The work permit application process differs based on whether Indian applicants are applying from inside Canada or from outside. A step-by-step application process for Canada Work Permit from India is given below.

Step 1: Be eligible to work in Canada

  • Get a valid and full-time job offer from a Canadian employer
  • or Complete your studies in a Canadian Institution through a Canadian Student Visa
  • Obtain an open work permit as a Spouse or common-law partner of a permanent resident, temporary foreign worker, or international student
  • Obtain a Temporary Residence Visa (TRV) valid for six months or more
  • Apply while waiting for your permanent residence application decision
  • Apply as a Refugee Claimant
  • Under the Intra-Company Transfer

In addition, you must satisfy the following criteria for eligibility:

  • have strong mental and physical health
  • not having any prior convictions
  • have enough money to support your family while living in Canada
  • not pose a threat to Canadian national security
  • not intend to work for a company that provides sexual services
  • not intending to work for a company that cannot employ foreign workers

Step 2: Gather Documents

Applicants must first gather the documents required for their Canada work permit application from India. This includes:

  • Proof of identity: Indian applicants must provide a valid passport or other travel document and two photos. They must also include pictures of any family members accompanying them.
  • Proof of Canadian employment: This includes an Offer of Employment Number for LMIA-exempt work permits. Other applicants will need to provide a copy of the Labour Market Impact Assessment from ESDC and their contract with their future employer. In addition, applicants must provide proof that they meet the jobs requirements.
  • Proof of relationship: Includes a marriage certificate and birth certificates for the family members accompanying the applicant.
  • Additional documents: This includes proof of present immigration status for applicants not currently residing in their home country.

Step 3: Start Your Canadian Work Permit Application Online

Next, Indian applicants must fill out various forms and applications on the Government of Canada website. These forms must be completed accurately and signed. Canadian Immigration will reject or return incomplete forms.

Step 4: Paying Application Fees

  • You can use a credit or debit card to make payments
  • Work permit Application fee: 155 CAD
  • Open work permit: 155 CAD + 100 CAD (Addional Fee)
  • The Biometrics Fee: 85 CAD

STEP: 5 Receive Approval Letter

Successful Indian applicants will receive an approval letter that specifies they are allowed to work in Canada. Applicants must present this letter at the port of entry when they attempt to enter Canada. They will then receive their work permit.

Step 6: Complete Your Biometrics

Digital photos and fingerprints are used during biometric identification. You schedule an appointment with a Canada Work Permit Application Center to provide your biometrics in this phase.

Step 7: Medical Examination

If you intend to stay in Canada for longer than six months, a medical exam is required. Medical professionals known as Panel Physicians are qualified to perform your immigration medical examinations.

Step 8: Move to Canada

After a visa officer has approved your TRV, you can fly to Canada and enter the country by showing your Canadian work permit visa at the airport.

Canadian Work Permit Fees from India

  • Work Permit Fee: 155 CAD/person
  • Open Work Permit: 255 CAD/person
  • PGWP Fee: 155 CAD + 100 CAD (Open Work Permit fee)
  • SOWP Fee: 155 CAD + 100 CAD (Open Work Permit fee)

Average Processing Time for Canadian Work Permit from India

The length of time it takes to process a work visa in Canada depends on a number of variables, including where you are applying from and the type of work permit you are applying for (open or employer-specific). Processing time for Canada Work Permit from India applications ranges between 1 and 14 weeks.

FAQs: Canada Work Permit from India

How long will it take to process Canada work permit from India?

IRCC may take anywhere from 1 to 9 months to process a work permit. Prospective workers are advised to apply for their work permit as soon as possible.

What is the general processing fee for the Canadian work permit?

The work permit processing fee is currently $CAD 155.

How long does a Canada work visa last?

There is no standard duration for the validity of a Canada work visa. Some are set to expire within 6 months of being issued. Others may be valid for multiple years.

Is proof of funds required for a Canadian work permit?

You may be required to provide proof of funds for certain types of work permits.
