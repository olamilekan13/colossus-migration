A Complete Guide to Self Employed Visa Canada from India

You can immigrate to Canada as a self-employed person through a Self-Employed Visa Canada from India. Self-employed artists, athletes, and applicants under eligible occupations can apply for the Self-Employed Persons Program.

Overview

Indian nationals interested in permanent residence in Canada can apply through various immigration programs. IRCC introduced the Self Employed Visa Canada to enhance the social fabric of its communities by creating a unique program targeting people from athletics and cultural activities. The Self-Employed Persons Program (SEPP) is best suited for specific types of self-employed Indian nationals.

The Self-Employed Visa Canada from India is preferable for individuals who may not qualify under the Federal Skilled Worker Program. It offers a straightforward pathway for permanent residency.

What is Self-Employed Visa Canada?

The Self-Employed Visa Canada specifically targets applicants who may not fit into the regular Federal Skilled Worker program because of the type of occupation they represent. If you take an example of a cricketer, a dancer, or a movie director, they might be doing some great work in their field and still may not qualify as per the work experience requirements for a skilled worker.

Some Indian applicants may qualify for both the Federal Skilled Worker Program and the Self-Employed Person’s Program.

Eligibility Requirements: Canada Self Employed Visa from India

Indian applicants must meet several requirements to be eligible under the Self-Employed Persons Program. This includes requirements for:

  • Eligible Occupations under Self-Employed Visa Canada
  • Work Experience Requirements
  • Self-Employment Requirements
  • Security, Medical, and Other Criteria

Work Experience

Indian self-employed applicants must possess the relevant experience to be eligible for this program. In this case, relevant experience refers to the experience acquired while working in athletics or cultural activities at a world-class level.

Individuals that have worked as self-employed professionals in athletics or cultural activities are also included in this definition.

Prospective Indian applicants must gain this relevant experience in the five years preceding their application. Applicants with experience in cultural activities must meet the following requirements:

  • Providing two examples of one-year periods in which the applicant was self-employed in cultural activities.
  • Providing two examples of one-year periods in which the applicant was participating in cultural activities at a world-class level.
  • A combination of the above two schemes, with one year of experience in each.

Applicants with experience in athletics must meet the following requirements:

  • Provide two examples of one-year periods in which the applicant was self-employed in athletics.
  • Provide two examples of one-year periods in which the applicant was participating in athletics at a world-class level.
  • A combination of the above two schemes, with one year of experience in each.

Self-Employment Experience

Applicants must possess relevant experience as self-employed individuals. Such applicants must also be willing and able to work as self-employed in athletics or art once they arrive in Canada.

Security, Medical, and Other Criteria

Indian applicants are required to undergo medical exams and security checks to ensure they are admissible to Canada. These applicants must also demonstrate that they possess enough funds to sustain themselves and their families while living in Canada.

Criteria to Meet Before Applying for Self-Employed Visa Canada

Applicants must meet three vital criteria before applying for the Self-Employed Persons Program. These are:

1. Points Requirements

This is the first step for Indian applicants who must score a minimum of 35 points out of 100 to be eligible to apply under this program.

2. Relevant Experience

Not all Indian applicants that come from sports or creative fields are eligible to apply under this program. Indian professionals must meet the relevant work experience requirements mentioned above.

They must also demonstrate that they have worked in a professional capacity in these fields. Such applicants may include their portfolios to showcase their work experience and achievements.

This requirement is critical, as Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) would prefer professionals who have worked in these fields at a high level. They should demonstrate through this portfolio that they are among the best in their respective field.

Prospective applicants can reach out to Colossus Migration for assistance with this. Our professionals can assess an applicant’s work experience and optimize their Self-Employed Visa Canada application portfolio.

3. Self-Employed Status and Business Plan

Indian applicants must be willing and able to become self-employed once they receive permanent resident status in Canada. They must demonstrate their willingness by including a comprehensive business plan with their application.

This business plan must include key details such as:

  • Applicant’s background and work experience
  • Where the applicant plans to settle in Canada
  • How do they intend to establish themselves as self-employed professionals in their field of work and become self-sufficient

A business plan also helps applicants demonstrate that they will contribute to Canada’s economy rather than become dependent on its social system. A great business plan should be able to convince a Canadian visa officer of this easily.

An applicant who cannot sufficiently demonstrate that they will be self-employed will be rejected, even if they meet all the other eligibility criteria. At Colossus Migration, we can help applicants create a robust business plan that meets the required standards.

Application Process: Self-Employed Visa Canada

Prospective self-employed Indians can apply for the Self-Employed person program using the following steps:

  • Obtain the Application Package
  • Gather Your Documents
  • Pay the Application Fees
  • Submit Your Application
  • Submit Your Biometrics and Photos
  • Receiving a Confirmation of Permanent Residence

1. Obtaining an Application Package

Indian applicants can obtain an application package for the Self-Employed person program from the Government of Canada website. They will need to complete the application form included and hit the “validate” button once it is complete.

2. Gathering the Required Documents

Indian applicants should then gather the documents mentioned in the document checklist included in the application package. This includes:

  • Language exam results
  • Education documents
  • Work experience documents
  • Passports and other travel documents

3. Paying the Application Fees

Applicants will then need to pay application fees. This includes:

  • Processing fees
  • Right of Permanent Residence fees
  • Biometrics fees
  • Other fees for third-parties

All the above fees must be paid online at the Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) website.

4. Submitting an Application

After completing each of the application requirements, applicants submit their applications to the concerned immigration department.

5. Sending Biometrics and Photos

Applicants will then be contacted and told to submit their biometrics. This includes items such as fingerprints and photos. Applicants will have 30 days to submit these once they receive the initial request.

6. Receiving a Confirmation of Permanent Residence

If successful, Indian applicants will receive a Confirmation of Permanent Residence along with an entry visa into Canada.
