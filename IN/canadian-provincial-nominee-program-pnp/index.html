What is the Canadian Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)?

Prospective Indian immigrants who wish to immigrate to Canada can apply through an applicable Canadian Provincial Nominee Program. It was introduced in 1998 to evenly fulfill the population and labor requirements of all the Canadian provinces.

Canada’s Provincial Nominee Program consists of over 80 immigration programs run by different provinces and territories in Canada. This program is designed for employees who have the skills, education, and work experience to contribute to a province’s or territory’s economy.

The Government of Canada sends out a certain number of invitations chosen from the pool of Canadian Provincial Nominee Program applicants each year. These provincial programs are popular because they offer prospective immigrants a speedy route toward receiving permanent residency.

Let’s learn more about the Canada PNP Programs in this comprehensive guide.

All Canada PNP Immigration Options

You can apply for permanent residence through the following Canada PNPs:

British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program (BC PNP)

British Columbias Provincial Nominee Programs are suited for foreign nationals who possess the skills to become contributing members of the provinces workforce.

Alberta Advantage Immigration Program (AAIP)

The province of Alberta offers numerous Provincial Nominee Program streams. The first two are for foreign workers presently working in Alberta or those who wish to work in the province.

Manitoba Provincial Nominee Program (MPNP)

Manitobas PNP consists of seven streams spread across Skilled Workers, International Education, and Business Investor categories.

Ontario Immigration Nominee Program (OINP)

The Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program serves as Ontarios PNP to assist foreign nationals in obtaining permanent residency.

Saskatchewan Immigration Nominee Program

Saskatchewans Immigrant Nominee Program includes the following three categories; International Skilled Worker, Saskatchewan Experience, and Entrepreneur and Farm.

New Brunswick Provincial Nominee Program (NBPNP)

New Brunswicks provincial nominee program was designed for foreign nationals who possess the necessary skills and background to contribute to the provinces economy.

Newfoundland and Labrador Provincial Nominee Program

The Newfoundland Labrador (NLPNP) nominee program consists of 6 different streams: Express Entry Skilled, Skilled Worker, International Graduate, and 3 more pathways.

Nova Scotia Nominee Program (NSNP)

The Nova Scotia nominee program consists of nine different streams. These are: Nova Scotia Labour Market Priorities, Labour Market Priorities for Physicians, Physician, Entrepreneur and 5 more pathways.

Prince Edward Island Provincial Nominee Program (PEI PNP)

Prince Edward Islands Provincial Nominee Program consists of the following streams; Critical Worker Stream, Skilled Worker Stream and, International Graduate Stream.

Yukon Provincial Nominee Program (YPNP)

Yukon offers the following Provincial Nominee Program streams; Critical Impact Worker, Skilled Worker, and Express Entry.

Northwest Territories Nominee Program (NTNP)

Canadas Northwest Territories offer two Provincial Nominee Program streams; Business Stream and Employer-Driven Stream.

Who should apply for Canadian Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)?

Each provincial nominee program targets specific types of individuals. This includes:

  • Skilled or Semi-skilled workers that possess a job offer
  • Individuals with past study or work experience in the province
  • Individuals that intend to work in occupations that are in demand under the PNP
  • Entrepreneurs

Types of Canada PNP Streams

Canadian Provincial Nominee Program falls under two broad categories:

1. Express Entry Aligned PNPs

Applicants applying through an Express Entry aligned PNP must have an active profile in Canadas Express Entry pool. This means that they should be eligible for one of the federal immigration programs, i.e. Federal Skilled Worker Program(FSWP),Federal Skilled Trades Program(FSTP)or the Canadian Experience Class(CEC). Those who manage to receive a nomination from their chosen province will receive an additional 600 points on their CRS score in the Express Entry pool. This greatly boosts their chance of receiving an Invitation to Apply(ITA).

2. Non-Express Entry Aligned PNPs

Applicants applying through the non-Express Entry aligned PNPs do not need an Express Entry profile when applying. This option is great for applicants who do not qualify for Express Entry.

Another way to categorize for Canada PNP

Canadian Provincial nominee programs can also be categorized into one of three types.

  • Job offer PNPs: These require applicants to have a valid job offer from a Canadian employer in their chosen province.
  • Past Study or Work PNPs: These programs are for applicants that have studied or have past work experience in the province.
  • Occupation in Demand PNPs: These programs are for applicants outside Canada working in occupations deemed in-demand by the province. Some of these programs may not require a job offer.

In addition to the above, different PNP streams can also be classified as:

  • First Come, First Serve: PNP programs operating on a First Come, First Serve basis accept and process applications in the order that they receive them. They continue accepting applications until their quota is met.
  • Expression of Interest: Applicants applying through the Expression of Interest PNP programs are required to first submit an Expression of Interest. In these PNP programs, they have to wait for an invitation from the respective province or territory based on the applicable eligibility criteria before applying for provincial nomination.
  • Base Streams: These PNP programs do not operate using the Express Entry system.

Canadian Provincial Nominee Program: Step By Step Process

Applicants can apply for their chosen provincial nominee program by first applying to their chosen province for a nomination. They must then submit a permanent residence application to the Canadian federal government. The steps for doing this are outlined below:

1. Determine PNP Eligibility

Applicants should first determine their eligibility for the PNP program they want to apply.

2. Complete a PNP Application

Applicants must then complete a PNP application and submit it to their chosen province or territory.

3. Receive the Provincial Nomination Certificate

Successful applicants will receive a provincial nomination in the form of a certificate.

4. Submit the Permanent Residence Application

Applicants can then apply for permanent residence through the Federal government. Applicants that have used a provincial nomination program aligned with the Express Entry system must apply through Express Entry.

FAQs: Canadian Provincial Nominee Program

Here are some frequently asked about about the Canadian Provincial Nominee Program.

Who is eligible for Canada PNP?

Each Canadian province has its own requirements for its PNP streams. These requirements are usually based on work experience, education, and language skills. Some streams may have additional requirements such as minimum funds, valid job offer, or the intention to open or acquire a business in the province.

How much CRS score is required for PNP Canada?

Saskatchewans International Skilled Worker stream offers the easiest immigration pathway for immigrants who speak English or French, possess one year of work experience, and have completed post-secondary education.

How long does it take to get PNP in Canada?

Receiving your nomination from a province or territory typically takes 60 days. The following steps can be completed within 8 months. This means it takes a total of 10 months to get PNP in Canada.

What is paper-based PNP?

The paper-based PNP process involves receiving a nomination certification from your chosen province or territory. Applicants can then apply for PR status via Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC)

Can you apply for PNP without a job offer?

Yes. Many PNP streams do not require applicants to possess a job offer. This includes:

  • Alberta PNP via Express Entry.
  • Saskatchewan Immigrant Nominee Program (SINP)
  • Ontario Immigration Nominee Program (OINP)
  • Quebec Skilled Worker Program (QSWP)
  • Nova Scotia Nominee Program (NSNP)
  • Prince Edward Island PNP via Express Entry
