Canada PR Process: Step-by-Step from India

The Indian applicants must follow these steps for the Canada PR process from India.

Step 1: Determine Your Eligibility

The first step towards getting Canada PR from India would be checking if you qualify for any immigration programs. Check the eligibility requirements for the Canadian Express Entry which is the most common Canad PR program from India.

Step 2: Verify your Foreign Education through ECA

To demonstrate equivalency to Canadian schooling, candidates must get an Educational Credential Assessment (ECA) certificate. This can be done with the help of some designated organizations like WES.

Step 3: Take IELTS or Equivalent Language Test

The candidates will have to pass a language test to demonstrate their command of any or both of Canada’s official languages, English or French. Only government-approved language tests must be taken by the applicant to obtain Canada PR from India.

Step 4: Create an Express Entry Profile

The candidates must create an Express Entry Profile and submit an application through one of the three federal economic immigration programs via Express Entry. Alternatively, they can apply for a Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) or other economic pilot programs.

Step 5: Get an Invitation to Apply

Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) will then rank candidates based on their program scores. Indian applicants can calculate their scores beforehand using our Express Entry Points Calculator. Only candidates who have enough CRS points must receive an invitation to apply for Canada PR from India. After getting an ITA the candidates get 60 days to prepare and submit a PR Application.

Step 6: Medical Exam and Police Clearance

Complete background and medical checks – The applicant for this position must get both a police clearance certificate and a medical certificate (PCC).

Step 7: Application Processing Stage

IRCC will assess your application for a Canada PR visa. If there are any further needs in this phase, the candidate must be prepared to give the requested information.

Step 8: Confirmation of PR

The candidate will be given a CoPR (Confirmation of Permanent Resident) at this stage following a positive assessment of their application.

Step 9: Move to Canada

After that, you simply, fly from India to Canada to become a permanent resident.

Canada PR Requirements from India

Canadas Federal Government introduced the Express Entry System in January 2015 as a replacement for the earlier first come, first serve application system. This system manages four essential immigration programs for Indians:

  • Federal Skilled Worker Program
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program
  • Canadian Experience Class
  • Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs)

The basic Canada PR requirements from India are as follows:

1. Language Skills

Each program within the Express System includes specific language requirements. Prospective Indian applicants must demonstrate their fluency in English or French by taking an approved Canadian Language Benchmark Test.

The minimum English or French language score required for each program is shown below:

  • Federal Skilled Worker Program:CLB 7
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program:CLB 5 in listening and speaking categories, CLB 4 for writing and reading
  • Canadian Experience Class: CLB 7 if the applicants NOC TEER Category is 0 or 1, CLB 5 if the applicants NOC TEER Category is 2 or 3.

2. Work Experience

Express Entry PR programs also require applicants to have a minimum work experience in specific occupations. These occupations are classified using a National Occupation Classification or NOC 2021.

The occupation type for each program is shown below:

  • Federal Skilled Worker Program: Canadian or foreign work experience in TEER 0, TEER 1, TEER 2 or TEER 3 jobs.
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program: Canadian or foreign work experience in TEER 2 or TEER 3 skilled trade jobs.
  • Canadian Experience Class: Canadian work experience in NOC TEER 0, 1, 2 or 3 jobs

The amount of work experience required for each program is shown below:

  • Federal Skilled Worker Program:One full year of continuous work in the ten years preceding the application.
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program:Two full years of work completed in the five years preceding the application.
  • Canadian Experience Class:One full year in the three years preceding the application.

3. Job Offer

The job offer requirements for each program are summarized below:

  • Federal Skilled Worker Program:Job offer not required. Indian applicants may be eligible for extra points if they possess a valid job offer.
  • Federal Skilled Trades Program:A valid job offer is required. This job offer must be for full-time employment that is a minimum of 1 year in length. Alternatively, Indian applicants can provide a Certificate of Qualification related to a skilled trade issued by the Canadian Government.
  • Canadian Experience Class:Job offer required.

4. Education

There are no education requirements for the Federal Skilled Trades Program and the Canadian Experience Class.

Indian applicants applying through theFederal Skilled Worker Programmust have completed secondary education or high school. These applicants can be awarded additional points if they have also completed post-secondary education.

Canada PR Points Calculation

The immigration process to Canada is based on Comprehensive Ranking System points. A point-based system enables newcomers to evaluate in advance whether they qualify for the Canada PR from India. Additionally, it streamlines and makes visible the entire process of applying for a PR visa from India.

In order to qualify for a Canada PR through the Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP), skilled immigrants must receive at least 67 out of a possible 100 points. The following selection criteria are used to determine how these points are awarded:

  • Language Ability (Maximum 28 points)
  • Education (Maximum 25 points)
  • Work Experience (Maximum 15 points)
  • Age Factor (Maximum 12 points)
  • Arranged Employment (Maximum 10 points)
  • Adaptability Factor (Maximum 10 points)

An applicant can create an express entry profile if they have obtained at least 67 points for Canada Immigration. These points are given under the CRS system:

  • Core Human Capital Factor
  • Transferable Skills Factor
  • Spouse or Common-law partner Factor
  • Additional Points

Canada PR Processing Time from India

Canada PR visa processing time from India varies with the type of application. It takes 6-8 months to process an application under the Express Entry system. But if you are applying from any other Canada PR Visa stream, like BC PNP, then it might take more time.

The candidates are encouraged to check the latest processing times for Canada PR from India, on IRCC’s official website.

FAQs: Canada PR from India

What is the difference between Express Entry and FSW?

FSW is a type of Express Entry program. It is one of the three main economic immigration programs of Canada.

What is the difference between FSW and PNP?

PNP is a province-focus immigration pathway, while FSW is valid all over Canada (except Quebec). If you apply for PNP in addition to FSW you will get 600 extra points on the nomination. Although you can apply for a standalone PNP program as well. Learn more.

How many CRS points are required for FSW in Canada?

You typically require a minimum of 67 points out of 100 if you wish to be eligible under the Express Entry System.

What is the general processing time for FSW in 2022?

Generally, it takes around 6 months for Express Entry applications to be processed. But FSW applications is expected to rise to 36 months in 2022 because of over-achieved targets as perthis planby IRCC.

What is sufficient proof of funds in FSW Program?

  • bank account statements in your name or in the name of the spouse/common-law partner accompanying you to Canada.
  • cashable investments that are in your name or in the name of the spouse/common-law partner accompanying you to Canada.
  • cashable fixed deposits that are in your name or the name of the spouse/common-law partner accompanying you to Canada.
