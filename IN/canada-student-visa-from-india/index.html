What is a Canada Student Visa?

A Canada Student Visa is a unique document issued to foreign students who wish to study at any designated learning institution (DLI) in Canada. To obtain a Student Visa for Canada from India,  you must apply for a Canadian Study Permit first. There is no age limit for a Canada student visa. Although you will have to prove your intentions to return to your home country after completing your studies. You may apply for a Study Permit after a Long Gap, with the help of a solid case file and adequate documentation, and avoid study permit refusal.

Why Study in Canada?

There are many reasons to consider pursuing higher education in Canada as an Indian student via a Canadian Student Visa. This includes:

1. World-Class Universities

Canada has many top-ranking universities that are at the forefront of different fields. This includes:

  • University of Toronto
  • Thompson Rivers University
  • York University
  • University of Winnipeg
  • Humber College

Indian students planning to study in Canada will have access to many exceptional programs. Employers typically seek out graduates with degrees and diplomas from universities, as mentioned earlier. This makes it much easier for Indian students to secure a great job after graduating. Book a consultation with our Canada Student Visa Experts to learn more about the higher education options in Canada.

2. Indian Immigrants are Welcome

Canada is known as an immigrant country that welcomes newcomers with open arms. The country possesses a rich and diverse culture. This makes it easy for anyone to fit in. Canadians are known for their warm and friendly demeanor, so new students will always feel welcome while studying in the country.

3. Affordability

Pursuing higher education in Canada can seem expensive, especially for prospective Indian students. However, the cost for Indian students studying and living in Canada is relatively affordable when compared to other first-world countries.

How Long Does It Take to Start Studying in Canada?

Prospective Indian students can start studying in Canada in as little as four months. This process starts by first applying for admission to a Canadian university or college. Most of these institutions divide their semesters into Fall, Winter, and Summer semesters. The starting periods for each of these semesters are shown below.

  • Fall Semester: Starts in early September.
  • Winter Semester: Starts in January
  • Summer Semester: Starts in April/May

Requirements for Canada Student Visa from India

You must generally meet the following requirements to be eligible for a Canadian student visa:

  1. You must be fully vaccinated.
  2. You must be enrolled in a Designated Learning Institution
  3. You must provide Proof of Financial Support to support your life and studies in Canada
  4. You must have no criminal record
  5. Good physical and mental health needs to be proved.
  6. Provide evidence to prove intention to leave Canada after completion of studies

The Total Cost of Study in Canada in 2023

The living expenses in Canada are approximately 10,000 CAD per year. Tuition fees may range from CAD 25-40K for Bachelor’s Degree Programs and CAD 25-75K for Master’s Degree Programs, depending on your program and chosen educational institution.

Document Checklist for Canada Student Visa from India

Indian students that wish to pursue higher education in Canada must obtain a Canadian student visa and a Canada study permit. A Canada study visa consultant can typically help with this process. A Canada student visa allows foreign students from India to enter Canada, while a study permit allows them to study at educational institutions there.

The required documents for a Canada Student Visa from India include:

  • A valid passport
  • Copies of your 10th, 12th, and degree certificates
  • Two academic references
  • Two employer references
  • A statement of purpose
  • Certificates related to extracurricular achievements
  • A valid letter of acceptance from an educational institution
  • Proof of application payment
  • Proof that you possess enough funds
  • Passport size photos
  • Proof of proficiency in English

Please reach out to the Canada student visa consultants at Elaar Immigration for additional clarification on any of the above requirements.

Working While Studying in Canada

Prospective Indian students over the age of 18 can work while studying in Canada under certain circumstances.
They can work:

  • On or off-campus for up to 20 hours per week during their semester without a work permit.
  • Full-time during winter and summer breaks without a work permit.
  • During co-op semesters using a co-op work permit if they are enrolled in a co-op or internship program.

Bringing a Spouse and Dependents to Canada While Studying

Indian students studying in Canada under a study visa can bring their spouses and children to the country. Their spouses can apply for a work permit and work in the country if their partner’s study permit is valid. Study permit holders can also bring dependent children to Canada for the duration of their study permit.

Settling in Canada After Studying

Plenty of Indian students settle in Canada after studying. This is because the government of Canada makes the path to permanent residency easy for international students.

Students can easily apply for a post-graduate work permit that allows them to work in the country legally. A post-graduate work permit can last for up to three years. This grants new graduates an opportunity to gain Canadian work experience and apply for permanent residency through the Express Entry system.

3 Steps to Apply for a Canadian Student Visa

Prospective students can apply for a Canadian Student Visa by following the steps below:

1

Select a Study Program and Apply to a University or College

Students must first choose the kind of course or program they want to enroll in. Then, they might look into several colleges and universities in Canada. If you want to increase your chances of getting accepted, think about applying to several programs and/or designated educational institutions.

2

Receive Your Letter of Acceptance

Students will get receive a letter of acceptance if they’re admitted to one of these authorized educational institutions. For the purpose of applying for a student visa, this letter is essential.

After receiving your acceptance letter, you must gather additional documents and submit a student visa application.

3

Gather the Required Documents and Complete the Application Process

Students need to gather extra paperwork and submit a student visa application after obtaining their acceptance letter. Upon clearance, you can take a flight to Canada to begin your education.

Canada Student Visa Application Process

The steps for applying for a student visa to Canada are listed below:

STEP 1: Determine Your Eligibility

The students must determine if they can fulfill the aforementioned eligibility requirements, out of which receiving a Letter of Acceptance is the top priority. Check if you are eligible for Canada Student Visa from India: FREE Evaluation Tool.

STEP 2: Prepare Your Study Permit Application

After that, students gather the application-related paperwork. They are listed in the Document Checklist for the Canadian Student Permit. Feel free to contact us if need help with your Canadian student visa application process.

STEP 3: Submit Your Canada Student Visa Application

Students can then fill out and submit a Study Permit application online or using a paper-based process. The online process can be completed at the IRCC website. Applications submitted via the paper-based process must be submitted to a Visa Application Center (VAC) in their country.

Then, students can complete and submit a Study Permit application, either online or on paper. Paper-based applications must be submitted to a regional Visa Application Center (VAC).

STEP 4: Wait for a Decision

Next, the students wait for the approval of their Canadian student visa. Depending on the nation you are applying from, a typical Canadian study visa takes 90 days (13 weeks) to be approved.

STEP 5: Move to Canada

Accepted students can take a flight to Canada. The Canadian student visa will be handed to you at the border crossing.

Canada Study Permit After a Long Gap

There is no upper age limit for Canadian Study Permit applications. It makes no difference if you are requesting a Canada study visa five, ten, or even twenty-five years later. For standard applications, the Canadian Study Permit already has high rejection rates. Due to their difference in age and education, many mature students are turned away.

Canada Student Visa Refusal Reasons

The most common reasons for Canadian Study Visa Refusal are mentioned below:

  • Failed to demonstrate that coming to Canada is not for studies
  • There is no intention to leave Canada
  • IELTS and other language proficiency issues
  • Fraudulent paperwork submitted for approval
  • Limited employment opportunities in the country of origin
  • Lack of strong family ties, insufficient personal assets, and a financial situation

FAQs: Canada Student Visa

Here are some common questions on the application process for Canada Student Visa from India.

How to study in Canada without a Student Visa?

Students who are already inside Canada can start a course of educational programs lasting less than 6 months without a study permit.

How long will it take to process the Canadian Study Permit?

Study permit applications submitted from outside Canada are usually processed within 12 weeks. Note that obtaining and submitting biometrics may lengthen the application completion and processing times.

Minimum requirements for a Canadian Study Permit

International students must be enrolled at a qualifying designated learning institution and possess a minimum amount of money to cover their tuition and living expenses while studying in Canada.

Exactly how much money is needed to Study in Canada?

You will generally need enough funds to cover your living expenses and tuition for the duration of your study in Canada. This amounts to approximately CAN$10,000 per year for living expenses, and anywhere from CAN$11,000 to CAN$50,000 per year for tuition.

Can I study in Canada for free?

You cannot study for free as an international student in Canada.
