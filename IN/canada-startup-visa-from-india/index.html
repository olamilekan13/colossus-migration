The Canada Startup Visa program is a special immigration program that allows qualified foreign entrepreneurs to settle in Canada and set up a startup business. This program was launched on March 31st, 2018 as a three-year program. It has since been made a permanent part of Canadas immigration programs. The Startup Visa Program offers a great alternative for Indian entrepreneurs who have an innovative business idea that can compete on a global scale and contribute to the Canadian economy and generate employment for the local people.

What is Canada's Start-Up Visa?

The Startup Visa Canada program is a one-of-a-kind program that caters to foreign entrepreneurs with a startup business proposal. Indian entrepreneurs who qualify for this program can enter Canada and settle after establishing their start-ups there.

If you want to apply for Canada Start-Up Visa from India you must know that it tends to be highly selective. The Canadian Government’s goal is to attract entrepreneurs that will contribute greatly to Canadas economy.

It is a great choice for business owners who do not qualify for the Federal Skilled Worker program or the other Provincial Nominee Programs (PNPs).

Eligibility Requirements for Canada Start-Up Visa from India

To apply for the Canada Startup Visa program, Indian entrepreneurs must meet the following eligibility requirements:

  • You must have a qualifying business
  • You must obtain a valid letter of support from a designated organization
  • You must meet minimum language requirements
  • You need to show enough proof of funds to live in Canada

1. You Must Have a Qualifying Business

The Canada Start-Up Visa applicant must start a qualifying business to be eligible. Additionally, the applicant must hold 10% or more of the businesss voting rights associated with its shares. If multiple applicants apply for the Canada Start-Up Visa from India, they must hold more than 50% of the voting rights combined.

In addition to meeting the above requirements, applicants must:

  • Be involved with actively managing the business from inside Canada.
  • Ensure a vital component of business operations is conducted in Canada.
  • Ensure the business is incorporated within Canada.

2. You Must Obtain a Valid Letter of Support from an Organization

Applicants must possess a valid letter of support via a designated organization. These organizations include venture capital funds, angel investor groups, and business incubators. Each of these organizations possesses different requirements. Therefore, applicants may need to pitch their business proposals to each one accordingly.

The minimum requirement investment required from each designated organization is shown below:

  • Venture capital funds: $200,000
  • Angel investor groups: $75,000
  • Business incubators: No requirement as long as the applicant is accepted into the Canadian business incubators program.

Indian entrepreneurs can learn more about the importance of creating a great business plan for applying for business immigration here. If successful, the designated organization will provide the applicant with a letter of support. This letter must then be included along with the Canada Startup Visa application.

3. You Must Meet Minimum Language Requirements

Indian entrepreneurs must also possess the ability to communicate fluently in English and/or French to succeed in Canada. To meet this requirement, applicants must demonstrate their language skills by taking a language test from an agency approved by the Canadian immigration department i.e., Immigration, Refugees, and Citizenship Canada.

This test assesses applicants on their language skills in:

  • Reading
  • Writing
  • Speaking
  • Listening

To be eligible for this Canada Startup Visa program, applicants must achieve a Canadian Language Benchmark 5 or higher on their test. All applicants must ensure they are fluent in English and/or French before applying for the Startup Visa program.

4. You Need to Show Proof of Funds to Settle in Canada

Applicants must also demonstrate that they possess enough funds to settle in Canada. These funds should cover living expenses for them and any dependents that will be coming to Canada with them.

Application Process: Canada Start-Up Visa

The application process for the Canada Startup visa program is straightforward. Applicants must first pitch their business idea to the designated organizations mentioned earlier. They must successfully convince them to support their venture. If a designated organization chooses to support the startup, it will send a commitment certificate to IRCC and the applicant a letter of support. Once all the eligibility has been met, applicants can complete a Startup Visa application and submit it.

The steps involved in the application process for Canada Start-Up Visa from India are:

Step 1: Fill Out the Canada Start-Up Visa Application Form

  • The Generic Application Form for Canada must be filled out
  • Complete all required fields on the form before clicking the “Validate” button
  • Include the barcode page in the printed application form once it has been validated
  • The form must be signed and dated

Step 2: Gather All the Required Documents

  • Check the [IMM 5760] Document Checklist to ensure that all documents are gathered in the order specified
  • Put all necessary documents in a sealed envelope
  • Take a printout of the document checklist and sign it
  • Your application should include this document checklist as a cover page

Step 3: Pay Start-Up Visa Application fees

  • The application fee includes processing fees for you and anyone you include on the application
  • The Right of Permanent Residence Fee (RPRF)
  • Fees for third-party services, such as medical exams, police certificates, and language tests
  • Biometrics fees

Step 4: Submit Your Canada Start-Up Visa Application

  • Make sure your application is finished before submitting it.
  • Ensure that you have signed all documents.
  • Pay your processing fees and submit your application with receipts.
  • Include all relevant documentation

Place all paperwork in a 9×12 inches envelope, label it “startup visa,” and send it to the specified location.

Processing Times

Processing times for Canada Start-Up Visa from India vary between 12 to 16 months. Note that securing a commitment certificate and a letter of support can take several months on its own. Applicants should plan their settlement in Canada with these processing times in mind.

Qualifying for Work Permits

Applicants that have entered Canada and are awaiting their application decision can work if they possess a work permit. These work permits are vital for Indian entrepreneurs to start working in their startup businesses quickly.

Canada immigration provides short-term work permits for such applicants if they possess a commitment certificate from a designated organization. In addition to this, applicants must pay an employer compliance fee and intend to live in a province or territory outside Quebec.
