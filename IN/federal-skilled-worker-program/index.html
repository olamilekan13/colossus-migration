What is Federal Skilled Worker Program?

The Federal Skilled Worker Program is one of three immigration programs under Canadas Express Entry system. It offers a viable immigration pathway for skilled Indian workers that wish to immigrate to Canada. In this step-by-step guide, we will discuss how to apply for the FSW Program from India.

The Federal Skilled Worker Program Eligibility Criteria

To be fully eligible for the Federal Skilled Worker Program, Indian applicants must meet requirements in the following areas:

1. Minimum Work Experience

  • Indian applicants must possess work experience in a qualifying occupation. These must fall under one of the TEER 0, TEER 1, TEER 2 or TEER 3 categories of NOC 2021.
  • Applicants must demonstrate that they performed the duties outlined in their lead statement in their primary occupation when applying. These include each of the essential duties and the majority of the primary responsibilities assigned.

Applicants must possess skilled work experience that was:

  • A paid position (unpaid internships or volunteer work do not qualify)
  • Completed continuously for at least one year or 1,560 hours.
  • Completed within the last ten years.

Indian applicants can achieve the 1,560 hours requirement mentioned above in the following ways:

  • Full-time position at one job: Working a minimum of 30 hours per week for 12 months
  • Part-time positions: Working less than 30 hours per week for several months until meeting the 1,560-hour minimum.
  • Full-time positions at multiple jobs: Working 30 hours per week for 12 months in more than one job.

2. Language Requirements for the FSWP

  • Applicants must meet the CLB 7 in Listening, Speaking, Reading, and Writing of the IELTS or equivalent tests.
  • These test results must not be more than 2 years old at the time of application.
  • Language proficiency tests can be taken in one of Canadas two official languages: English and French
  • It must be from a designated institution approved by IRCC.

3. Education Requirements for Federal Skilled Worker Class

  • Indian applicants may be eligible for this program if they studied in Canada and possess a degree, diploma, or certificate from a Canadian high school or a postsecondary institution.
  • Indian applicants who have completed their education outside Canada must possess a recognized completed credential and an Education Credential Assessment from a designated organization.
  • This Education Credential Assessment shows the applicants education is equivalent to a completed degree, diploma, or certificate from a Canadian high school or post-secondary institution.

4. Proof of Funds

  • Indian applicants must demonstrate that they possess enough funds to settle in Canada. This includes funds for themselves as well as any family members that will be accompanying them.
  • Applicants who are legally allowed to work inside Canada and possess a valid job offer from a Canadian employer in the country are exempt from this requirement.

Selection Factors for the FSW Program

Federal Skilled Worker Program applicants get assessed against the FSW Eligibility Point Grid System. They have to obtain at least 67 points out of 100 on IRCC’s six selection factors given below:

  • Education: Up to 25 points for formal education.
  • Language Skills: Up to 28 points (24 first official languages, and 4-second official languages).
  • Work Experience: Up to 15 points for paid skilled work experience in the last 10 years, but at least 9 points are required to qualify.
  • Age: Up to 12 points.
  • Arranged Employment (Valid Job Offer): Up to 10 points if the candidate has a valid job offer in Canada.
  • Adaptability: Up to 10 points for a number of factors to show that the candidate is adaptable to successfully settle in Canada.

Step-by-Step Process for Federal Skilled Worker Program

The Canada Express Entry steps for applying through economic immigration programs are outlined below.

Step 1: Determine Eligibility for FSWP

To apply for the FSW Express Entry Program, you must meet the Federal Skilled Worker Class Requirements listed above. Start your assessment here to see whether you are qualified.

Step 2: Education Assessment (ECA)

If you have completed an Education Credential Assessment (ECA) through one of the authorized organizations, you are eligible to claim FSW points for your education. WES is one of the most well-known organizations with ECA issuance rights.

Step 3: Language Proficiency Test

You must take exams like the IELTS, CELPIP, or equivalent French language tests in order to receive FSW points based on your language proficiency.

Step 4: Express Entry Profile Creation

You will receive enough FSW points after getting your ECA and language exam results. After that, you can set up an Express Entry profile for the Federal Skilled Worker Program.

Step 5: Get an ITA

Only applicants who surpass the Latest FSW Draw cut-off score are given an ITA. You have 60 days to prepare and submit an electronic Application for Permanent Residence after being granted an Invitation to Apply (ITA) (eAPR).

Step 6: Medical Exam and Police Verification (PCC)

After that, in order to proceed to the next stage of the application process, Federal Skilled Workers must pass a medical examination and police clearance stage.

Step 7: Application Processing Stage

At this phase, IRCC process your application. If additional documentation is needed to submit an application for the Federal Skilled Worker Program, a visa officer contacts you at the application processing stage.

Step 8: Confirmation of Permanent Residence

The Canadian government makes the final decision on your FSW application and you receive a Confirmation of Permanent Residence (COPR).

Step 9: Move to Canada

The new permanent resident can then apply for a permanent residence card. This card shows proof of their status when traveling outside the country.

FAQs: Federal Skilled Worker Program

What is the difference between Express Entry and FSW?

FSW is a type of Express Entry program. It is one of the three main economic immigration programs of Canada.

What is the difference between FSW and PNP?

PNP is a province-focus immigration pathway, while FSW is valid all over Canada (except Quebec). If you apply for PNP in addition to FSW you will get 600 extra points on the nomination. Although you can apply for a standalone PNP program as well. Learn more.

How many CRS points are required for FSW in Canada?

You typically require a minimum of 67 points out of 100 if you wish to be eligible under the Express Entry System.

What is the general processing time for FSW in 2022?

Generally, it takes around 6 months for Express Entry applications to be processed. But FSW applications is expected to rise to 36 months in 2022 because of over-achieved targets as perthis planby IRCC.

What is sufficient proof of funds in FSW Program?

  • bank account statements in your name or in the name of the spouse/common-law partner accompanying you to Canada.
  • cashable investments that are in your name or in the name of the spouse/common-law partner accompanying you to Canada.
  • cashable fixed deposits that are in your name or the name of the spouse/common-law partner accompanying you to Canada.
