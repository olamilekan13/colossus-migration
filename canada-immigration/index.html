Canadian Immigration Background

Immigration has a long history in Canada. Indigenous peoples of Canada worked alongside European settlers to create the nation. Canada has welcomed millions of immigrants from all around the world ever since the Federation in 1867. “A Canadian is an immigrant with seniority.All Canadians, except the country’s Indigenous population, are either immigrant themselves or their relatives.

Why does Canada Support Immigrants?

The amount of people working (sometimes referred to as the labour force) and paying taxes to support our public services, such as health care, is one indicator of how strong the Canadian economy is.

The labour force in Canada is still expanding each year. Without immigrants, Canadian employers would struggle to fill open positions with suitable candidates. This is a result of Canadians living longer lives and having fewer kids. This is why Canada will always support immigrants now and in the future.

Canadian Immigration Pathways

Canada has planned to 500,000 new immigrants per the new Immigration Levels Plan 2023-2025. IRCC offers a fast, flexible and dynamic immigration system for immigrants. There are more than 90 potential immigration pathways to Canada for eligible skilled professionals, entrepreneurs, and families. You can learn more about your options in this detailed guide. Whether you are applying from outside or inside Canada, you must fall under one of the following categories of Canadian immigration:

  • Canadian Skilled Immigration
  • Canadian Business Immigration
  • Family Sponsorship Visa
  • Moving from the USA

Who can apply for Canadian Immigration?

  • Foreign Skilled Workers, Entrepreneurs and Graduates and Post-Graduates studying in Canada.
  • Foreign Skilled Workers and International Students have a valid job offer from a Canadian employer.
  • Family of a Canadian Permanent Resident or Citizen.
  • Refugee Applicants

Skilled Immigration to Canada

Many foreign skilled workers are moving to Canada worldwide for better employment opportunities and quality of life. These individuals help flourish the Canadian economy. Thus Canada welcomes thousands of skilled immigrants each year. Most of these immigrants immigrate to Canada through one of the following Canadian Skilled Immigration pathways.

Federal Express Entry Programs

IRCCs Express Entry System is one of the most popular options for immigrating to Canada. It consists of the following three programs:

  • Federal skilled Workers Program (FSW)
  • Canadian Experience Class (CEC)
  • Federal Skilled Trades (FSTP)

The Express Entry System is popular because applicants can apply based on their work experience, education, and language skills. You wont need a job offer to be eligible for these programs if you prove you have enough funds to support yourself.

Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)

The Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) does not require applicants to possess a job offer. These programs are specific to each province in Canada, and their requirements tend to vary from province to province. The PNPs for some provinces invite candidates to the Express Entry pool of applicants. Other provinces assess candidates based on the number of points scored under the Expression of Interest (EOI) points system. Each PNP Program has categories targeting foreign skilled workers, international students and business owners.

Check out: British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program

Atlantic Immigration Program

The Atlantic Immigration Program (AIP) is the name of Atlantic Canada’s immigration programme (AIP). New Brunswick, Nova Scotia, Prince Edward Island and Newfoundland and Labrador are the four provinces that make up Canada’s Atlantic Region.

Quebec Immigration

Immigrants who wish to settle in Quebec can choose one of the many Quebec immigration pathways the province offers. The selected applicants under Quebec Immigration programs receive a Quebec Selection Certificate, or Certificat de slection du Qubec (CSQ). Each of these programs has distinct requirements. Prospective applicants should study each one to ensure they qualify before applying.

Other Economic Immigration Pilots

Canada also provides a few economic immigration pilot programs targeting specific Canadian regions and in-demand occupations. These programs are operated at the federal level, and applicants can apply for permanent residence upon approval.

  • Rural and Northern Immigration Pilot (RNIP)
  • Agri-Food Pilot Program
  • Caregiver Visa Canada
    • Home Child Care Provider Program
    • Home Support Worker Pilot Program

Canada Investment Visa & Business Immigration

IRCC also offers Canada Investment Visa and Business Immigration programsfor professionals with skills that would benefit the Canadian economy. Most of these business immigration programs allow foreign entrepreneurs and investors to obtain Canada PR by investment. These business immigration options fall under various types of programs, including:

  • Business-Focused PNPs
  • Self-Employed Visa Canada
  • Canada Start-Up Visa Program
  • Intra Company Transfer Canada

Family Sponsorship Visa

The Canadian government helps family members of permanent residents and citizens to live in Canada permanently. Family Sponsorship Visaprograms are available to spouses, common-law partners, parents, grandparents, and dependent children of Canadian citizens and permanent residents.

  • Spousal Sponsorship
  • Parent and Grandparent Sponsorship
  • Dependent Child Sponsorship

To sponsor your family, you must sign an “undertaking.” This implies that the person you will sponsor will depend financially on you. You could have to pay it back, for instance, if they need social assistance.

The Canadian Super Visa is another option for parents and grandparents of permanent residents and citizens.

How to Find the Right Canadian Immigration Program?

To find a suitable Canadian immigration program, you must go through the following steps:

STEP 1: Determine a suitable immigration category

You can immigrate to Canada and apply for permanent residency as an entrepreneur, international student, temporary foreign worker or a family member of a Canadian permanent resident or citizen. Determine which immigration category suits best for your profile. Start your immigration assessment here.

STEP 2: Start an Application Online or Offline

You can apply for a particular Canadian Immigration program online through the IRCC official portal or start a paper-based application. It is advised to consult a Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant to represent your case.

STEP 3: Submit the Application and Wait for Approval

After completing and successfully submitting an application, you must wait for the processing. It can take from 12 to 26 months for an application to process, depending on the Canadian immigration pathway you choose.

STEP 4: Receive an ITA

Once you get approval, IRCC will invite you to apply for permanent residency by sending you an ITA. You must submit your Canada PR application within 60 days of receiving an Invitation to Apply.

STEP 5: Get Confirmation of Permanent Residency

You get an email from IRCC mentioning a Confirmation of Permanent Residency (CoPR). This is the final step before you can move to Canada, or if you’re already in Canada, you get the freedom to stay permanently without worrying about your status. It takes about 45 days to process your PR card via mail.

FAQs: Canadian Immigration

Here are some frequently asked questions about Canadian Immigration.

What is the fastest way to immigrate to Canada?

The Canadian Experience Class is the fastest program for prospective immigrants. Applications under CEC can be processed in as short as three to four months.

How long will it typically take to get PR in Canada?

The amount of time it takes to get your PR will vary depending on your chosen program. Applications processed via the Express Entry System are typically the fastest.

Which province in Canada gives PR easily?

The amount of time it takes to receive your PR varies based on many factors under the Provincial Nominee Program. Saskatchewan, Alberta, Ontario, and Nova Scotia are believed to have the easiest PNP requirements.

How much money is required for Canada PR 2022?

The amount of money required for Canada PR varies depending on your chosen program. For example, you need a minimum of $13,200 if you are applying through the Federal Skilled Worker or Federal Skilled Trades Program.

What program is best for Canada Immigration?

The best program varies depending on your qualifications and background. Please get in touch with our experts to learn which program offers you the easiest immigration pathway.

How to immigrate to Canada from the USA in 2022?

You may be eligible to immigrate to Canada from the USA if you qualify for any of the three programs listed under the Express Entry System.

Candidates may also be eligible to apply through the Provincial Nominee Programs, Family Sponsorship, or via one of the Business Immigration pathways.
