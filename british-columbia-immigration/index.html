British Columbia Immigration Guide: How to Move to BC?

In this British Columbia Immigration guide, we will review the province’s main BC PNP streams and categories and outline the application steps.

Canada is one of the most diverse places on Earth, both demographically and topographically. However, no Canadian province is quite like British Columbia. This province has massive mountain ranges that intersect with the sea, expansive forests, and geographically unique islands of various sizes.

The province also has a bustling economy and is home to some of Canada’s biggest agriculture, film & television, and manufacturing industries. All these factors make British Columbia a top destination for immigrants. It is estimated that 83,000 new immigrants made British Columbia their new home between 2021 and 2022.

If you’re interested in British Columbia immigration, you may wonder where and how to start your search. In this BC immigration guide, we will go over the main immigration programs for this province and outline the application steps.

British Columbia Immigration: Who Can Apply?

There is an official British Columbia immigration program for each of the following types of people:

  • International students and their families
  • Foreign skilled workers and their families
  • Foreign entrepreneurs and investors, and their families

BC PNP Skills Immigration

The BC PNP Skills Immigration program is suited for foreign professionals in skilled or semi-skilled jobs in high demand in British Columbia. It also contains special streams for international students that have studied in British Columbia or other Canadian provinces.

  • BC PNP Skilled Worker: This stream is open to people with two years of work experience and who also have a full-time job offer in NOC TEER Categories 0, 1, 2, or 3 from a British Columbia employer.
  • BC PNP International Graduate: This stream is for international students who graduated from a Canadian College or University and have a full-time job offer in NOC TEER Categories 1, 2, or 3 from a British Columbia employer.
  • BC PNP International Post-Graduate: This stream is for international students with a Master’s degree or a Ph.D. in health, applied science, or natural sciences.
  • BC PNP Healthcare Authority: This stream is suited for foreign professionals with a full-time job offer from a British Columbia Public Health Authority body.
  • BC PNP Entry-Level and Semi-Skilled: This stream caters to entry-level or semi-skilled workers currently employed in the province.

Processing Time: The BC PNP Skills Immigration stream has an average processing time of approximately 4 months.

BC PNP Express Entry

The BC PNP Express Entry is a special subcategory within the Skills Immigration stream. This one caters to people with an active Express Entry profile and who are in the Express Entry candidates pool. This means the applicant must be eligible for at least one Canadian Federal Express entry program.

The BC PNP Express Entry categories are:

  • Express Entry BC Skilled Worker: This stream is open to people with two years of work experience and who also have a full-time job offer in NOC TEER Categories 0, 1, 2, or 3 from a British Columbia employer.
  • Express Entry BC Health Authority: This stream is suited for foreign professionals with a valid recommendation letter from a British Columbia Public Health Authority body
  • Express Entry BC International Graduate: This stream is for international students who have graduated from a Canadian College or University and have a full-time job offer in NOC TEER Categories 1, 2, or 3 from a British Columbia employer.
  • Express Entry BC International Post Graduate: This stream is for international students who obtained a Master’s degree or a Ph.D. in the health, applied science, or natural sciences field.

Application Fee: The application processing fee for each BC Express Entry stream is $1,150.

Processing Time: The BC PNP Express Entry immigration stream has an average processing time of 2 to 3 months.

BC PNP Entrepreneur Immigration

The BC PNP Entrepreneur Immigration program contains streams for business people and entrepreneurs with the means and willingness to invest in BC businesses and operate them. The streams in this category are:

  • BC PNP Base Category: This stream is for people with experience in business ownership or management. Candidates must have a minimum net worth of $600,000 and be willing to invest a minimum of $200,000. They must acquire at least 33.3% of the company and create one or more jobs for Canadians.
  • BC PNP Regional Pilot: This stream is for people with experience in business ownership or management who have scored a minimum of CLB in a language proficiency exam. Candidates must have a minimum net worth of $300,000 and be willing to invest a minimum of $100,000. They must acquire at least 51% of a company in a city with a population of less than 750,000 and create one or more jobs for Canadians.

BC PNP Tech

The BC PNP Tech is a special stream for foreign tech workers with the skills and experience needed to work in the province’s tech sector. Prospective BC PNP Tech applicants must possess valid Canadian immigration status, have a valid job offer from a BC employer, and score a minimum of CLB 4 in a language proficiency exam.

Application Fee: BC PNP Tech program candidates will need to pay a $1,150 application fee.

Processing Time: The total processing time for BC PNP Tech is 2-3 months.

Application Fee: BC PNP Entrepreneur Immigration candidates will need to pay a $300 registration fee and a $3,500 application fee.

Processing Time: The total processing time for BC PNP Entrepreneur immigration is 6 months.

British Columbia Immigration Process

The steps to apply for British Columbia immigration are described below.

Step 1: Identify the Right BC PNP Pathway

The first step is to identify the right British Columbia immigration pathway. Review all the programs and streams described above and check which ones you are eligible for. If you believe you are eligible for more than one, consider speaking with a Regulated Canadian Immigration Consultant to determine which offers you the best chance of success.

Step 2: BC PNP Registration

Next, you must create your online profile at the BC PNP Online Portal. Applicants applying through the Health Authority or Post-Graduate stream are exempt from doing this step.

You will then be entered into a candidate pool. You will receive an official Invitation to Apply (ITA) if selected.

Step 3: Apply for BC PNP Application Online

You will then have 30 days to complete the BC PNP application and submit it online. You must also gather and submit all the required documents and pay your application fee.

Step 4: Obtain a Provincial Nomination from British Columbia

You will receive a provincial nomination if the British Columbia authorities approve your application.

Step 5: Apply for Permanent Residence

Once you have your nomination, you can apply for Canadian permanent residence through the Express Entry system.
