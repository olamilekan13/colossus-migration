Canada Express Entry Latest Draw September 19, 2023 ~ 3,200 Invitations

By Kawal Sharma19 September 2023No Comments
Share Tweet Share Pin

On September 19, 2023, the first Express Entry round of invitations was held by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC). A total of 3,200 candidates were issued invitations in an all-program draw, with a minimum Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score of 531 required to receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA).

Express Entry draws were unexpectedly paused for a month, and IRCC has not given a reason. However, on September 18, they announced that the draws would resume this week.

The current pool’s CRS Cut-Off has experienced a 35-point increase, from 496 to 531, as a result of the recent pause. Unfortunately, this development has been disappointing for the waiting candidates.

Canada Express Entry Draw September 19, 2023 Results:

  • Express Entry Round: #263
  • Draw Type: All-Program
  • Draw Date: September 19, 2023
  • CRS Cut-off Score: 531
  • No. of invitations: 3,200
  • Tie-breaking rule: September 12, 2023

What is Express Entry?

The Express Entry system is used to manage applications for Canada’s Federal Skilled Worker Program, Federal Skilled Trades Program, and Canadian Experience Class.

Each program has specific eligibility criteria, but all applicants are assessed and ranked based on the Comprehensive Ranking System. This takes into account factors such as language proficiency, age, work history, occupation, and education. Those with the highest CRS scores have a greater chance of being invited to apply for permanent residency in Canada.

Please note: If you receive a provincial nomination via the Provincial Nominee Program (PNP), you’ll get an advantage in Express Entry draws as you’ll automatically receive an additional 600 CRS points.

Category-Based Express Entry

On May 31, IRCC introduced category-based selection draws, which target specific sectors such as healthcare, STEM professions, trades, transport, and agriculture. These draws prioritize candidates who have relevant work experience or a strong French-speaking ability.

The aim of these category-based selection draws is to address urgent job vacancies in in-demand sectors of Canada’s workforce and strengthen the economy. However, candidates in the Express Entry program still need to have a high CRS score to be competitive in these draws.

With the recent announcement of the resumption of Express Entry draws, it remains to be seen how the selection process will unfold in the coming weeks.

How to Increase Your CRS Score and Receive an Invitation

If you’re looking to improve your CRS score and receive an invitation to apply (ITA) for Canadian permanent residency, there are several alternative approaches you can consider:

1. Targeted Express Entry

Consider the Targeted Express Entry pathway, which offers invitations at a lower CRS score. Seek guidance from immigration experts to determine your eligibility and receive the proper assistance.

Read more:Targeted Express Entry 2023 Guide

2. PNP Immigration

Explore provincial nominee programs (PNPs) to significantly raise your CRS score. Research and select a program that aligns with your skills and qualifications.

Read more:PNP vs. Express Entry: Which is Better for You?

3. LMIA-Based Job

Get a job offer in Canada that’s backed by a Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA). This will help you gain valuable experience and improve your CRS score.

Learn more:How to get a job offer from outside Canada?

4. Study Permit and PGWP in Canada

Choose a Canadian Study Permit Visa that allows you to gain work experience through the Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) program. This route can eventually lead to permanent residency through the Canadian Experience Class (CEC).

Also read:Canada Study Visa After 5 to 25 Years Gap

5. Visitor Visa to Work Permit

You can achieve a transition from a visitor visa to a work permit in Canada by finding employment with a Canadian company or exploring work opportunities through personal connections.

Also read:Visitor Visa to Work Permit in Canada

6. French Language

Boost your CRS score by up to 50 points by attaining proficiency in French. Being a French speaker provides advantages in Targeted Express Entry Draws and opens doors to permanent residency opportunities for Francophone immigrants.
