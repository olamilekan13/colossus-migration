Five Canadian Universities ranked among Worlds Top Universities!

By Keshav Sharma Blog, Canada Study Visa No Comments

The Times higher education world rankings for 2021 have been released, and Canada has 5 Universities in the top 200 and 3 Universities in the list of top 50 Universities. While the 3 in the leading 50 are the University of Toronto, which ranks 18th, at 34th position is the University of British Columbia, and McGill University is at 40th position in the list.

There are numerous World University rankings announced every year, and every one of them changes depending on the features offered by the Universities. At the same time, the Times positioning spotlights more on research and teaching. Times rankings rate universities in 5 primary classifications, to be specific; research, income generated, teaching performance, international reputation, and journal citations in the education business.

University of Toronto

The University of Toronto is positioned at number 18 and is one of the best among the Canadian Universities, barring the typically prevailing American and British Universities. The University is positioned second after the Swiss Federal Institute of Technology. Unlike these top Universities in America and the United Kingdom, who get substantial financial donations and gifts from the private associations, the Canadian University system is competitive, educational expenses are comparatively low, and funding is received solely from the Government.

The University offers more than 700 undergraduate courses and around 200 master’s courses. The University is best known for its practices in the medical stream. The University of Toronto ranks 18th because of its “academic reputation” and is famous for its research and innovations. The University is the birthplace of stem cell and insulin research.

All in all, ten notable personalities completed their education from the University of Toronto, including five of the Canadian Prime Ministers and writers Margaret Atwood and Michael Ondaatje.

University of British Columbia

British Columbia is a situated in the western part of Canada, famous for its breath-taking environment and soothing views. The University of British Columbia marks to be on the second position in Canada and 34th in the list. From 2016, the University has climbed 11 positions by achieving the highest rank for academic reputation. The University has around 64,800 students on its Vancouver campus, where 12 streams are offered. Another campus is in Kelowna, where seven streams are offered.

The University of British Columbia is the ancient and also one of the most competitive universities in Canada. Many scholarships are available for students taking admission in the University of British Columbia, including the Donald A. Wehrung International Student Award for applicants who belong to war-torn nations.

Eight famous individuals who won Noble prize are associated with the University of British Columbia, 65 individuals from Olympic medallists and 71 who are Rhodes scholars. The current Canadian Prime Minister, Justin Trudeau, completed a bachelor’s degree in education faculty from the University of British Columbia. Two more Prime Ministers of Canada graduated from the University of British Columbia.

The University of British Columbia and the University of Toronto marks the top two positions, as they are famous for delivering high-quality education. Along with these Universities, Canada’s other Universities welcome international students, and cosmopolitan culture is encouraged.

McGill University

McGill University which is in Montreal ranks 40th in the list. In the year 1821 the University was founded and currently, it accounts for around 39,660 students. McGill University is the only University that made its way to the World Economic Forum’s Global University Leaders Forum, which incorporates 26 top higher education universities and institutes.

McGill offers degrees and recognitions in more than 300 fields of study, with the most important Canadian college admission requirements. Most students apply for the five most significant streams offered by McGill University, to be specific Science, Arts, Medicine, Management and Engineering.

With 30 percent of students coming to McGill from more than 150 nations, its student body is the most universally diverse for medical-doctoral research stream in the country. McGill is positioned among the best 50 colleges globally and among the leading three colleges in Canada. It has held the top situation for from past 15 years in the yearly Maclean’s Canadian University Rankings for medical-doctoral faculty.

McMaster University

McMaster University is based in Hamilton, Ontario and ranks 69th in the list among the top 200 Universities in the world. The McMaster University was founded in 1887 and is named after Honourable William McMaster, a prominent Canadian Senator and banker who donated 900,000 Canadian Dollars for the university’s foundation.

Popular with the tag of the most research-intensive, medical-doctoral University, McMaster University is known for many programs you can become a part of! A one-hour drive from Toronto and a one-hour by flight from major American airports New York City, Chicago, and Washington D.C., the university campus is situated in the core of one of the most lively and multicultural regions of Canada.

McMaster University is most famous for its clinical school; however, it deserves equivalent acknowledgment for creating innovative request based ways to deal with teaching typified by its Arts and Sciences program. In the remarkable Integrated Sciences program, a limit of 60 students studies mathematics and science in an interdisciplinary and undertaking based condition.

Canada’s first female space traveller and the first neurologist in space, Roberta Bondar, completed her degree from McMaster University.

University of Montreal

The University of Montreal has 10,000 international students, making it Canada’s third most global University, as specified by the Times Higher Education ranking. Every year, approximately 7,000 Canadian permanent residents enrol for courses at the University of Montreal.

The University of Montreal ranks fifth in the list, and more than 36,000 students enrol in the University. The University of Montreal offers 600 study programs. The University of Montreal draws in over $500 million for research funding each year, making it one of Canada’s third-best university research centres.

Numerous eminent laboratories were established at the University of Montreal, including MILA, the world’s driving college research centre in profound learning; the Institute for Research in Immunology and Cancer (IRIC), one of the most profitable in its space; and the Center de recherch en Droit public (CRDP), the most far-reaching public-law research centre in Canada.

Self-Employed Canada Immigration Program for Athletes and Creative Artists

By Keshav Sharma Blog, Immigration No Comments

Scenario 1: Amit Shah is an Indian cricket player representing India at national and international levels. He plans to retire and immigrate to Canada for a better future for himself and his family. Being an international cricket player, Amit is eligible to immigrate to Canada under the self-employed persons Canada immigration program as he can contribute to Canadians’ athletic life and economically. Being from an athletics or sports background, Amit does not need to qualify for the regular skilled worker category used by doctors, engineers, lawyers, marketers, teachers or other professionals qualified to immigrate to Canada. The self-employed Canada Immigration program is devised explicitly for creative or athletic professions which do not fall into the regular nine to five occupations. It will help Amit secure his future by obtaining Canada PR. Canada would get a sportsperson of international repute who can help Canadians in sports and contribute to the economy along the way. In this case, Amit can apply for the self-employed persons Canada immigration program and make his dream come true.

Scenario 2: Melissa is a Chinese professional international singer and performs at various international events organized by companies. As Melissa’s has been performing at the international level for the last many years, she intends to change her career by opening a music academy in Canada. It’s a vast market as a large number of Chinese immigrants are settled in Canada. Melissa is wondering if there is any specific program for artists such as herself who don’t fit into the regular skilled worker category of Canada Immigration. Here, Melissa can apply for Canada immigration under the Self-Employed persons immigration program to fulfil her dreams. Melissa’s plan to open up her singing academy in Canada will allow her to make a significant contribution to Canadian society in the field of music. Additionally, her academy will also contribute to the economy through direct or indirect taxes and the generation of employment. It’s a win-win both for Melissa and Canada!

Canada is a multicultural society with immigrants from all over the world, calling it their new home. As immigration in different occupations and trades is essential to keep the Canadian businesses and economy thriving, the Government of Canada also understands that certain occupations do not fit into the regular skilled, semi-skilled or unskilled jobs. Still, they are vital for the overall fabric of Canadian society. Hence, the Canadian Government has devised this specific program to encourage creative artists and athletes to immigrate to Canada. You can reach out to our licensed Canada immigration consultants to see if your occupation is eligible under this program.

The Self-Employed Persons Canada immigration program allows eligible candidates to immigrate to Canada and obtain Canada PR. These individuals should have relevant experience in athletics or cultural activities by which they can make a significant contribution to Canada’s cultural life or sports.

There are three essential components to this self-employed persons program:

  1. Attaining at least 35 points out of 100 to be eligible to apply for this program.
  2. Validating your experience in the given occupation or field is national/international through a comprehensive portfolio.
  3. Demonstrating through a proper settlement and business plan, how would you sustain yourself in Canada as a self-employed person.

Through the above components, one has to convince the visa officer that you will contribute to Canada’s economy by your cultural activity. The contribution depends on your occupation and the province you select.

Self-employed persons program is usually perceived as a business immigration program meant for individuals who run their own small-medium business, which is not valid. This program neither requires huge net worth nor any investment into a Canadian company. Self-employed doesn’t mean that you have to establish your business in Canada and show massive capital for Canada immigration. That’s the misconception. Instead, self-employed here means the candidate can cover their expenses by their self-employed activity. Here, “their” means the candidate himself/herself, their partner and dependent children. This program is intended for writers, authors, photographers, directors, artists, musicians, athletes and so on who can immigrate to Canada and engage in any self-employment activity to sustain and grow with their family for a better life.

In both the above scenarios, the individuals have created a platform for themselves in their cultural/athletic field. For such individuals, applying under the self-employed persons Canada immigration pathway is the best way to immigrate to the country to utilize their skills and safeguard their future.

If your occupation is eligible under the self-employed program, you should contact a regulated Canadian immigration consultant to know more. Your profession may also belong to the list, yet you do not qualify for a self-employed program. Don’t forget to check the eligibility and selection criteria for the self-employed Canada immigration program.

The self-employed persons program is a federal immigration method, which means the candidate intends to settle in any Canadian province, except Quebec. The self-employed Canada immigration program is one of the most challenging Canadian immigration pathways. What are the Steps for the Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)?

By Keshav Sharma Blog No Comments

The Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) is one of the major pathways for Canada immigration. Provincial Nominee Program results from an agreement between the federal government of Canada and respective provincial governments. Since each province and territory understand their labour needs better than anybody else, the federal government allows them to select and nominate candidates as per their requirements. It is important to note here that provinces can only nominate candidates; only Canada’s federal government can grant permanent residency. Depending on each province’s size and industry requirements, provinces are allocated an annual quota for nomination and accordingly, provincial governments create and manage their respective provincial nominee programs.

The Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) points out those skilled workers, independently employed individuals and business owners who can help develop that province’s job market. In Provincial Nominee Program (PNP), each region helps Canada to grow the economy, which profits the Canadians and, in return, offers social security benefits to the immigrants.

The main interest of the Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) is to invite the skilled workforce having required credentials such as education, age, work experience, and language proficiency in at least one of the official languages, i.e. English or French. If one satisfies the program’s eligibility criteria, you may receive the nomination from the respective Canadian province, except Quebec.

What after being invited by the Canadian province?

You earn extra 600 points once you are acknowledged by the PNP or issued the nomination certificate. Now, your CRS score will be among the highest in the pool of candidates waiting for the invite from IRCC. Consequently, in the next draw of Express Entry, you’ll be invited to apply for a Canada PR visa. Once you successfully receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA), you have 90 days to submit your documents and other requirements such as proof of fund, medical certificate, etc. You must finish the document verification and submission in 90 days. In case a delay is caused, you might lose the opportunity of applying for your Canada PR.

Step by step guide to apply for PNP

STEP 1: Knowing your Eligibility

Before applying for PNP, it is pertinent to understand if you are qualified for the same. Suppose you plan to apply for the British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program (BCPNP) or PNP of any other province or territory. In that case, you must check the eligibility criteria for each and determine which one applies to you. Also, remember that Quebec has its policies and eligibility criteria. Once you have selected the most appropriate PNP program for yourself, start collecting all the documents required for the application process. Do keep yourself updated on any changes as the prerequisites change without prior notice, and hence it’s essential to maintain a continuous check while applying.

STEP 2: Starting your Application Process

There are two kinds of provincial nominee programs, i.e., those linked to the Express Entry system and others those are stand-alone provincial programs. The ones related to the Express Entry system primarily target experienced skilled workers who are eligible for any federal immigration programs such as the Federal Skilled Worker Program, Federal Skilled Trades Program or the Canadian Experience Class. The other provincial nominee programs are targeted towards applicants required by the provinces such as business owners, semi-skilled and unskilled workers, etc.

The provinces invite the applicants through the Express Entry stream as well as non-Express Entry streams. Depending on your unique situation, your profile might be picked up from the Express Entry pool. You may get an invitation to apply by a particular province such as Ontario or Alberta. In such a case, you first have to apply for the respective PNP, followed by a PR application to the federal government through the Express Entry system. There might be another scenario wherein you are already in the Express Entry pool, and you apply at the PNP portal of provinces such as Saskatchewan and Nova Scotia. In the third scenario, you already work in a particular province or territory, and you can apply to the relevant provincial program in that province only. This program might be linked to the Express Entry system or a stand-alone provincial program. Once a province nominates you in all the above cases, you need to apply to Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) for a Canada PR visa. It is mandatory to submit your medical reports to prove that you are fit and healthy during the application process.

STEP 3: Knowing the Application Processing Time for Different Provinces

The IRCC sets different processing times for various Canada immigration programs. Hence, it is valid to know how much time your application will require to process. Before 2015, the processing times were not exact, as there was no online system. But now, you’ll get to check the processing time of your application on the online platform.

The application processing time is less for Express Entry linked PNP as compared to the non-Express Entry PNP. The application processing time is different for each province. One can check the application start time, closing time, and processing time on the province’s official site.

STEP 4: After Presenting your Application

Once you submit your Provincial Nominee Program application, you have to follow specific guidelines. Firstly, you’ll receive an acknowledgment of your application. You will also receive a letter saying, “the administration is evaluating your application.” If you submit your application, which lacks a few qualification criteria, your application will not pass.

If you have applied through a regulated Canada immigration consultant, such as Colossus Migration, your profile will be up to the mark. If you qualify for the PNP, you will receive an invite from the respective province. Once you receive the approval, you need to apply with IRCC for your Canada PR application.

The Canada immigration authority might request you to submit further documents required to issue you an invite. Make sure you submit the documents before the end date. Once you get the province’s nomination, you’ll score extra 600 points if you have applied via the Express Entry linked stream.

STEP 5: Arranging your Travel to Canada

If you apply for your PR application from outside of Canada, now is the time to live your desired life in Canada. Before travelling to Canada, you might contact a few companies/non-profit organizations that help new immigrants settle in Canada. These organizations help you after landing in Canada. For the same, you can contact Colossus Migration to know more about post landing services in Canada.

Once you land in Canada, your Canada PR card would be mailed to your home address in a couple of months. The Canada PR card will be your entity in the country and hence keep it safe and close to you.

A keynote here is that you must land and settle in the province which nominated you. Although the Canadian Charter of Rights provides the Right to Mobility to every individual, it is your moral duty to reside in the province that nominated you unless there is a compelling reason to move to another part of Canada.

The provinces that participate in PNP are Ontario, British Columbia, Alberta, Saskatchewan, New Brunswick, Manitoba, Nova Scotia, Newfoundland and Labrador, Prince Edward Island, Northwest Territories and Yukon. Each of these provinces has its PNP criteria, and hence it is recommended that before applying to any province, visit the provinces official website.

What Are Myths And Misconceptions Of Express Entry?

By Keshav Sharma Blog No Comments

The Express Entry Canada immigration program is one of the best ways to get Canada PR. Even though people claim that they are all aware of the Express Entry program, most people do not fully understand it. There are few misconceptions about the Express Entry program, which we at Colossus Migration will clear out in this quick read.

#1. Myth: It is mandatory to have a job offer to be eligible for Express Entry.

Fact: It is not necessary to have a job offer. Having one will help improve your score and chances.

The facts confirm that Canadian companies will be permitted to have a more direct effect through Express Entry. Yet, you won’t need an employment offer to move to Canada under the system.
Each candidate receives specific points in the Express Entry pool and is ranked using the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS). Under this system, you could get up to 1,200 points. Out of 1200 points, one could get 50 or 200 points depending on the NOC code of the qualifying job offer or 600 points for a provincial nomination certificate.

#2. Myth: No language test is required to enter the pool of Express Entry.

Fact: All the applicants should pass the language proficiency test, which is recognized by the Government of Canada, before entering the Express Entry pool.
All the applicants have to submit proof of language test results, which is confirmed by CIC. An applicant should either have English or French proficiency test results to enter the pool of Express Entry.

The language proficiency test score should meet the eligibility criteria, as mentioned by IRCC, for your selected federal economic immigration program. Hence, it is mandatory to take a language test if you are willing to enter the Express Entry pool.

LANGUAGE APPROVED TESTS FOR CANADA IMMIGRATION
English IELTS (Accepted: IELTS General training and not accepted: IELTS Academic for Express Entry)
CELPIP (Accepted CELPIP General test and not accepted CELPIP General-LS test for Express Entry)
French TEF Canada: Test devaluation de francais
TCF Canada: Test de connaissance du francais

#3. Myth: The eligible occupation list under the Federal Skilled Worker Program despite everything still applies.

Fact: There is a lot of confusion from potential applicants around the subject of the older eligible occupation list for the Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP) and whether it stays in place or will be updated. There is currently no eligible occupation list at all.

Instead, applicants should prove they’ve had a year’s job experience in a skilled occupation at some point in 10 years. This is mostly uplifting news for potential applicants who were not included in the past eligible occupations list regarding eligibility.

Canadian jobs fall under National Occupational Classification (NOC) codes, which are separated by expertise type and level, so you will have the option to check whether your occupation is named skilled.

Remember that the ineligible occupation list for Canadian Experience Class will also not apply to Express Entry.

#4. Myth: For being a Canadian economic immigrant, the only way is by applying under the Express Entry Canada immigration program

Fact: Most of the federal economic immigration programs come under Express Entry. However, the Canadian provinces can select the candidates from outside of the Express Entry System in a particular allocation.

Every province in Canada can select candidates based on the labour market requirement. The provinces can choose the number of immigrants under the Provincial Nominee Program (PNP). Under PNP, the provinces select the candidates who need not qualify the Express Entry eligibility criteria. It means that even though most economic immigrants will have to apply under the Express Entry system, including a few particular allocations, each territory and province will still have a base PNP.

For instance, Quebec is not a part of the federal Express Entry System. The immigrants have the option to apply under the Skilled Worker channel and a Quebec Experience program.

#5. Myth: One can enter false information while applying under the Express Entry system.

Fact: If you enter false information about yourself and get caught, you will be penalized. Do not lie in your application.

While applying for Canada immigration, no matter how many documents you need to submit, you cannot take the risk by lying on your immigration application. May it be you require time to collect all your records or attain few extra credentials before getting the ITA. Maybe you are just hoping you might not get caught, and then indeed, your future is at risk as you might get debarred forever from applying for Canada immigration.

The Government of Canada is stringent while checking and processing the immigration applications and the officials are vigilant to detect false information. The penalty for giving incorrect information and misrepresentation has increased from two years to five years of inadmissibility and a 5-year ban for applying for Canada PR.

#6. Myth: Once you get the Invitation to Apply (ITA) for Canada PR under the Express Entry system, to gather the documents, you’ll have lots of time.

Fact: It will be a hassle in collecting your required documents if you wait for your ITA, which you need to submit within the 60 days.

It takes time to gather all the required documents for your Canada immigration under Express Entry. You have to be ready with your documents, educational certificates, qualification credentials, employment reference letter, and other necessary documents to support your application.

#7. Myth: Whoever wants can enter the Express Entry pool.

Fact: If you are eligible for Canada immigration through the federal economic immigration program, you can enter the Express Entry pool.

It is the biggest misconception. People often think that anyone can immigrate to Canada under the Express Entry Canada immigration program. However, this is so not true! For immigrating to Canada under Express Entry, the candidate has to be eligible for one of the existing federal economic immigration programs, which are the Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP), the Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSWP), and the Canadian Experience Class (CEC).

#8. Myth: Under the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS), the applicants can get to know their scores and rankings and the number of points they require for an invitation to apply (ITA).

Fact: The applicants can get to know their Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) total points, but they’ll not get their specific ranking. The applicants will get to see the recent draw’s threshold points, but they will not know the total points required for the next Express Entry pool draw.

The CRS is for CIC’s benefit, an accurate technique to calculate the eligible applicants’ rank. CIC does not specify the upcoming draw score, but the qualified applicants can check the previous scores. Using this information, an applicant would know how many points he/she should aim. Although there is no guarantee of the points for the draw you are seeking, which will help you obtain your ITA for Canada PR under the Express Entry Canada immigration program.

#9. Myth: Express Entry is the most comfortable option for immigrating to Canada.

Fact: The Government of Canada is stringent while checking and processing the immigration applications, and one must submit all the supporting documents to meet the eligibility criteria as per the CIC’s standards.

The Government of Canada is striving hard to improve its immigration system, which will overcome Canada’s labour market shortage and increase the economy. For this, the processing time of the immigration application has shortened, but that does not mean your application won’t get checked. Your application will be reviewed meticulously. Hence, all the documents submitted should be valid, and the information presented should be accurate.

Once your Express Entry Canada PR gets approved, you have to move to Canada within the stipulated timeline given by IRCC. If you wait for a longer duration, your acceptance letter will be at risk.

Employment Reference Letter for Canada PR

By Keshav Sharma Blog No Comments
Share Tweet Share Pin

Are you applying for Canada PR under the Express Entry Program or the Atlantic Immigration Pilot Program or any of the other Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)? If yes, then you may require to submit proof of your current or previous work experience.

The candidate must submit a letter to Immigration, Refugee, and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) when the candidate is applying for Canada PR. The applicant must give a letter from the organization with all the specific information.

This letter is called an Employment Reference Letter. The blog provides an overview of how IRCC expects the letter to be and what one can do if their organization is not ready to give this letter.

Points to be included in your Employment Reference Letter

While applying for Canada PR, IRCC expects the letter to be in a proper format which includes the following points

  • The letter should be printed on the organization’s letterhead and should consist of the organization’s contact information.
  • The contact information should include the organization’s email address, phone number, and complete address.
  • The letter should mention the candidate’s supervisor’s details, including its name, designation, and signature. If the supervisor is not available, the Human Resource manager of the organization can do the needful.
  • It is mandatory to mention the candidate’s name, the title of the job, list of duties, the start date of the job and the end date of the job (if applicable), total working hours per week, salary, and added benefits received every year from the organization.
  • According to the guidelines of IRCC, having the organization’s stamp is not essential.
  • The crucial part of the Employment Reference Letter is to mention the exact duties and responsibilities.

Receiving the Employment Reference Letter from the Organization

Being open-minded, IRCC considers specific scenarios.

If the candidate fails to provide the Employment Reference Letter as per the mentioned guidelines of IRCC, it does not mean that the candidate’s application will not be considered.

The candidate has to provide other necessary relevant documents and a Letter of Explanation (LoE), which specifies that the candidate could not give the Employment Reference Letter as per the requirement of IRCC. By doing this, the candidate’s application could be considered.

If the Human Resource manager refuses to give the Employment Reference Letter, the candidate can have a word with the reporting Manager.

Remember to keep proof by sending an email to the human resource manager. Suppose they refuse to give the Employment Reference Letter. In that case, the candidate will have proof of email, which he can add to the supporting documents mentioning it as proof of the company refusing to give the required Employment Reference Letter.

If this does not work, the candidate can ask his or her reporting manager to sign the Employment Reference Letter on behalf of your organization and even without using the organization’s letterhead.

Make sure you include all the necessary information in this letter. The candidate has to add a copy of the company card and have their signature notarized.

After the manager signs the Employment Reference Letter, the candidate should add copies of relevant documents, including a job contract, offer letter, payslips, salary certificate, promotion letters, and so on.

How to use NOC to Draft the Employment Reference Letter?

It is essential to find the NOC (National Occupational Classification) code. Each NOC code mentions the list of responsibilities and duties. The candidate must search for the right NOC code, which matches their actual job duties and responsibilities.

It is one of the most critical parts of your immigration process. If you face any difficulty finding the right NOC code for your occupation, please consult a licensed Canadian immigration consultant. The candidate has to complete the majority of the job duties mentioned in the NOC code.

  • The candidate must write his duties by taking inspiration from the NOC code description.
  • One should not use the same order of the employment responsibilities and the same number of employment duties, as mentioned in the NOC code description.
  • It is advised that one should add two to three more tasks, which are not mentioned in the NOC code description.

Make sure you are not copying the NOC code page’s duties and paste them into your Employment Reference Letter.

IRCC will get to know the reliability and genuineness of your letter. Depending on the same, further investigation might consume time and extend the application process time and may end in rejecting the reference letter.

All you should know about Duolingo English Proficiency Test.

By Keshav Sharma Blog, Canada Study Visa, Immigration No Comments

Till now, the only accepted English tests in Canada for international students were IELTS Academic, TOEFL or CELPIP. Because of COVID-19, the applicants are affected by IELTS or TOEFL test centre closures. But most Canadian colleges and universities are temporarily started accepting Duolingo English test for qualifying the English language proficiency criteria.

Duolingo English proficiency test is like a blessing in disguise for international students as it will not affect their dream of studying in Canada.

WHAT IS THE DUOLINGO ENGLISH TEST?

Duolingo was launched in 2012 with a mission to make learning more accessible. In 2014, Duolingo came up with their English Language Proficiency test as an alternative to TOEFL and IELTS. Similar to IELTS, Duolingo consists of four sections, which are Reading, Speaking, Listening and Writing. The Duolingo English Test is an English language proficiency tool developed and designed for international students. It comprises the applicant’s English score, writing sample and video interview. It is a very user-friendly tool with a secure testing experience.

The Duolingo test is of 60 minutes, which costs $49. The cost comprises of unlimited test score reports required for University admission. The Duolingo English test consists of a series of questions wherein, after answering a question correctly, the difficulty of the next question increases comparatively. Similarly, if a question is answered incorrectly, the next question is relatively easy and straightforward. This feature of Duolingo makes it a comprehensive English test.

In the Duolingo test, the students have to differentiate between right and wrong English words, complete the missing sentence or words, describe an image, read sections verbally, respond to the text written in the section, and type the sentence they hear.

At the end of the Duolingo test, a video interview of the applicant is conducted wherein the applicant has to speak and write on any one topic from the two given topics.

After finishing the test, the applicant receives an email in the next 48 hours. The email contains the link which the applicant can share with the University. The Duolingo test marks a score on a scale of 10 to 160 points, which contextualizes skill levels and can be compared with other English proficiency tests. The minimum score of Duolingo English test depends on the criteria put forth by the University.

DUOLINGO TEST REQUIREMENTS

  • Your passport, driving license or Government ID
  • 60 minutes of time
  • A reliable internet connection
  • A reliable computer or laptop satisfying following
  1. A supported browser
  2. A microphone
  3. Clear audio speakers
  4. A front-facing camera

PREPARING FOR THE DUOLINGO TEST

A Duolingo sample test will help you be familiar with the question types you will encounter while giving the final Duolingo English Test. The sample test will help you know what to expect on the real test day, which will help you give your best on the final test day. The Duolingo sample test is free, and you can take it till how much ever time you want. Once you complete the Duolingo sample test, the test provides an estimated range of your score.

Note that the Duolingo sample test is only eight minutes shorter than the actual test, and the score range is less accurate and unofficial. Once you are prepared with the sample test, you can register for the final Duolingo test.

Once you finish the Duolingo test, a proctor will analyse your test video to confirm that you followed the test rules. The Duolingo test rules consist of not using earphones, not interacting with anyone else, not using your phone, iPad or other devices, and not looking off-screen. It is essential to follow these rules during the test, or you will need to retake the test again. The complete test rules are outlined in the Duolingo FAQs, and are built-in in the guidelines at the beginning of the Duolingo test.

DUOLINGO TEST PROCESS

The Duolingo English Test comprises of a writing portion and video interview. Both are counted in when you share your Duolingo results with a university. It is the best opportunity for international students to showcase yourself and your English language proficiency. The applicant has a choice of two question topics for each video interview and the writing sample. Then the applicant will speak for one to three minutes and write for up to five minutes. Do not worry as there is a timer that will keep track of you. If you get stuck, do not get frustrated, take a deep breath, and answer each question as best as you can. The applicant will be able to review your responses before you share your results with universities. If you are not happy with your performance, you can retake the test.

UNDERSTANDING THE DUOLINGO TEST RESULTS

The Duolingo English test score is out of 160 in 5-point increments. Test results include an overall rating as well as sub-scores of Conversation, Production, Literacy and Comprehension.

  • Conversation The applicant’s ability to speak and listen
  • Production The applicant’s ability to speak and write
  • Literacy The applicant’s ability to write and read
  • Comprehension The applicant’s ability to listen and read

The overall score of the test is not an average of the sub-scores. All score for all sections is calculated independently and are weighted combinations of the questions which contribute to the score.

The certificate of the Duolingo test result includes “Your Score Range,” which highlights the standard error of measurement (SEM).

If you are willing to apply to a Canadian institute or University which accepts and considers the Duolingo test score, then it is an excellent choice to give the Duolingo test.
