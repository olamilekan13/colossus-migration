5 Benefits of Moving to BC as an Entrepreneur

By Keshav Sharma Blog, Canada Study Visa No Comments

Canada is often considered as one of the best countries to move to operate a business. Its economy is continually expanding, and there is a great labor pool of skilled workers for business owners to choose from. If you are an entrepreneur, you should consider moving to British Columbia to pursue your business interests.

Here are five smart reasons why you should move to B.C. as an entrepreneur.

1. Great Economy

Most Canadian provinces have witnessed a fair amount of growth in the past decade. However, British Columbia’s economic growth between 2010 and 2019 stands out for being exceptionally great. According to this report there was a total of 532,500 businesses in British Columbia in 2019. Of these, 98 per cent (523,800) were small businesses with fewer than 50 employees. Entrepreneurs who wish to conduct business activities in a province that will benefit from high growth should look into British Columbia.

2. Numerous Industries

British Columbia is home to many thriving Canadian industries. This includes:

  • Construction
  • Agriculture
  • High technology
  • Mining
  • Manufacturing
  • Film & Television

Entrepreneurs seeking to break into any of the industries mentioned above will be able to make great strides by moving to B.C.

Know More: BC’s Fastest Growing Industries

3. Great Labor Pool

Entrepreneurs that move to a new country and start a business should be rightfully concerned about the labor pool available. You may have the funds to begin operating, but you may not have access to the right workforce to grow your business and take it to the next level.

The good news is that entrepreneurs in B.C. have access to a large pool of skilled workers. After all, many students come to B.C. from all over the world and study at its institutions. Such workers are highly educated and are eager to participate in its economy.

4. Low Taxes

Entrepreneurs that wish to move to a province with low taxes will be making the right choice by choosing B.C. The province features competitive corporate tax rates and enjoys the lowest provincial income taxes in the country. These low tax rates can improve your profit margins and help your business grow relatively quickly.

5. Low Cost of Living

In addition to focusing on business costs, entrepreneurs should also be concerned with living costs in their chosen location. Many large cities in North America, such as Los Angeles and San Francisco, are known for their high living costs. Such costs can make it difficult for entrepreneurs to get settled and pursue their business goals.

The good news is that the cities in British Columbia feature relatively low costs of living. The cost of living in B.C.’s largest city, Vancouver, is still lower than living in a megacity such as New York.

How to Move to BC as an Entrepreneur?

Prospective entrepreneurs interested in moving to British Columbia can use one of the numerous business immigration options available. These include:

Entrepreneur Immigration

The BC Entrepreneur Program is suited for entrepreneurs who can create a successful business in the province and improve its economy. This program selects candidates using a scoring system. Prospective entrepreneurs can look up their scores using our BC PNP points calculator.
To be eligible under the entrepreneur immigration program, entrepreneurs must meet the following requirements:

  • Possessing a net worth of $600,000 or greater.
  • Investing at least $200,000 in a qualifying new business or purchasing and improving an existing one in the province of B.C.
  • Creating one or more new full-time jobs for Canadian citizens and residents.
  • Possessing business management experience.
    Being eligible for temporary immigration to Canada.

Entrepreneur Immigration Regional Pilot

The Entrepreneur Immigration Regional Pilot program was launched in 2019 and is open until March 2024. This program is also suited for entrepreneurs that wish to start a business in Canada. However, unlike the main entrepreneur immigration program, the BC Regional Pilot Program is meant for entrepreneurs that wish to contribute to specific small communities in British Columbia.

To be eligible under the Entrepreneur Immigration Regional Pilot program, entrepreneurs must meet the following requirements:

  • Possessing a net worth of at least $300,000.
  • Offering business investments of at least $100,000.
  • Owning a minimum of 51% of the new business.
  • Achieve a Canadian Language Benchmark score of level 4 in English or French.
  • Creating one or more jobs for Canadian citizens and residents.
  • Performing an exploratory visit to the community, you will be conducting business activities within.

Strategic Projects

Prospective entrepreneurs interested in moving to British Columbia can use one of the numerous business immigration options available. These include:

  • Being well-established and remaining within good financial standing.
  • Demonstrating readiness to invest and expand in B.C.
  • Offering economic benefits for the province through a large investment.

Company employees can be eligible for this program if they meet the following requirements:

  • They are senior employees who possess the right qualifications and experience needed to work in their role in B.C.
  • They have been appointed in senior managerial positions or executive positions or possess specialized knowledge needed to run the company’s B.C. branch.

All you should know about Duolingo English Proficiency Test.

By Keshav Sharma Blog, Canada Study Visa, Immigration No Comments

Till now, the only accepted English tests in Canada for international students were IELTS Academic, TOEFL or CELPIP. Because of COVID-19, the applicants are affected by IELTS or TOEFL test centre closures. But most Canadian colleges and universities are temporarily started accepting Duolingo English test for qualifying the English language proficiency criteria.

Duolingo English proficiency test is like a blessing in disguise for international students as it will not affect their dream of studying in Canada.

WHAT IS THE DUOLINGO ENGLISH TEST?

Duolingo was launched in 2012 with a mission to make learning more accessible. In 2014, Duolingo came up with their English Language Proficiency test as an alternative to TOEFL and IELTS. Similar to IELTS, Duolingo consists of four sections, which are Reading, Speaking, Listening and Writing. The Duolingo English Test is an English language proficiency tool developed and designed for international students. It comprises the applicant’s English score, writing sample and video interview. It is a very user-friendly tool with a secure testing experience.

The Duolingo test is of 60 minutes, which costs $49. The cost comprises of unlimited test score reports required for University admission. The Duolingo English test consists of a series of questions wherein, after answering a question correctly, the difficulty of the next question increases comparatively. Similarly, if a question is answered incorrectly, the next question is relatively easy and straightforward. This feature of Duolingo makes it a comprehensive English test.

In the Duolingo test, the students have to differentiate between right and wrong English words, complete the missing sentence or words, describe an image, read sections verbally, respond to the text written in the section, and type the sentence they hear.

At the end of the Duolingo test, a video interview of the applicant is conducted wherein the applicant has to speak and write on any one topic from the two given topics.

After finishing the test, the applicant receives an email in the next 48 hours. The email contains the link which the applicant can share with the University. The Duolingo test marks a score on a scale of 10 to 160 points, which contextualizes skill levels and can be compared with other English proficiency tests. The minimum score of Duolingo English test depends on the criteria put forth by the University.

DUOLINGO TEST REQUIREMENTS

  • Your passport, driving license or Government ID
  • 60 minutes of time
  • A reliable internet connection
  • A reliable computer or laptop satisfying following –
  1. A supported browser
  2. A microphone
  3. Clear audio speakers
  4. A front-facing camera

PREPARING FOR THE DUOLINGO TEST

A Duolingo sample test will help you be familiar with the question types you will encounter while giving the final Duolingo English Test. The sample test will help you know what to expect on the real test day, which will help you give your best on the final test day. The Duolingo sample test is free, and you can take it till how much ever time you want. Once you complete the Duolingo sample test, the test provides an estimated range of your score.

Note that the Duolingo sample test is only eight minutes shorter than the actual test, and the score range is less accurate and unofficial. Once you are prepared with the sample test, you can register for the final Duolingo test.

Once you finish the Duolingo test, a proctor will analyse your test video to confirm that you followed the test rules. The Duolingo test rules consist of not using earphones, not interacting with anyone else, not using your phone, iPad or other devices, and not looking off-screen. It is essential to follow these rules during the test, or you will need to retake the test again. The complete test rules are outlined in the Duolingo FAQs, and are built-in in the guidelines at the beginning of the Duolingo test.

DUOLINGO TEST PROCESS

The Duolingo English Test comprises of a writing portion and video interview. Both are counted in when you share your Duolingo results with a university. It is the best opportunity for international students to showcase yourself and your English language proficiency. The applicant has a choice of two question topics for each video interview and the writing sample. Then the applicant will speak for one to three minutes and write for up to five minutes. Do not worry as there is a timer that will keep track of you. If you get stuck, do not get frustrated, take a deep breath, and answer each question as best as you can. The applicant will be able to review your responses before you share your results with universities. If you are not happy with your performance, you can retake the test.

UNDERSTANDING THE DUOLINGO TEST RESULTS

The Duolingo English test score is out of 160 in 5-point increments. Test results include an overall rating as well as sub-scores of Conversation, Production, Literacy and Comprehension.

  • Conversation – The applicant’s ability to speak and listen
  • Production – The applicant’s ability to speak and write
  • Literacy – The applicant’s ability to write and read
  • Comprehension – The applicant’s ability to listen and read

The overall score of the test is not an average of the sub-scores. All score for all sections is calculated independently and are weighted combinations of the questions which contribute to the score.

The certificate of the Duolingo test result includes “Your Score Range,” which highlights the standard error of measurement (SEM).

IS DUOLINGO FOR YOU?

If you are willing to apply to a Canadian institute or University which accepts and considers the Duolingo test score, then it is an excellent choice to give the Duolingo test. There is only one way to score best in the Duolingo test. You need to take the practice test and see if you score well. The best advantage here is that you can retake the Duolingo practise test as many times as you want. Once you are all set with the practice test, you are good to register for the final Duolingo English proficiency test.

Feeling disheartened, one of her friends in New Delhi connected her with Keshav Sharma of Colossus Migration.

“I had so many questions for him. For so many years, I had tried to find people who could help me to study in Canada or the UK,” she says. “My first question was, ‘At my age, you think I could get admission to a good university?’ and his answer was, ‘Why not?’. For so many years, I’d been asking this question.”

Keshav explains that every application is different, and while Deepanjali’s was unique, he felt they could achieve her goals. Understanding the complexity involved is vital as there can be no cookie-cutter approach; every aspect of the case must be reviewed carefully.

“It’s important to know your goals in the immigration process,” Keshav says. “She was clear about what she did and didn’t want, making it easier to identify the best immigration paths to consider. The bottom line is if there is a strong enough reason and a genuine story, then one can study at any age.”

The process was lengthy (about eight months), but together, they managed to get Deepanjali’s paperwork. In the interim, she took the IELTS (English language proficiency) exam and considered different universities.

When the exam results were in and were found to be satisfactory for a master’s degree level of study in Canada, she applied to five universities in Canada from a shortlist created by Colossus Migration from all available options – not just a few possibilities.

Keshav and his team crafted the list based on institutions that were the best fit in terms of Deepanjali’s education, background, work experience, the program offered, and immigration options if she’d like to pursue this at a later stage.

“I didn’t have a clue that the paperwork took that much time,” she says. “I had to get my transcripts done. Not an easy task in New Delhi. I got everything in place, and it took at least three months.”

Keshav explains that the process can take a while for complex applications. It’s important to be ready to gather the required information accurately.

“He has been not just a positive person,” she says of Keshav’s guidance, “He has been actually like a mentor. He guided me well.”

Her first acceptance came from the University of Prince Edward Island. While the family had suggested she might not like the winter climate, Deepanjali felt relief. Even if no other university accepted her, she had one of her five choices as an option. Now, it was a matter of securing a Canada student visa and seeing if her other choices came through.

“Then I got accepted from Thompson Rivers University [TRU],” she says. “Everyone spoke so well of Kamloops. I was so happy. After living such a hectic life.”

She called Keshav, who was just as happy about her news as she was. They completed some more forms, and within two weeks, they submitted her Canada student visa application.

“It was like someone who helps you, who walks you through everything,” she says of Keshav. “Every little thing, he would call me, he would advise me.”

The next step was to go for biometrics with a visa facilitation service in New Delhi. Once complete and Colossus Migration submitted her visa application online, it was a matter of waiting, again, to see if Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) would approve her request for the study visa. It would take about two weeks for the answer. This was the step that others had told her would be impossible, and she wasn’t looking forward to waiting.

Yet, as Deepanjali explains, there was some special kind of magic at play.

Within just 12 hours of submission, Keshav called her to say he had received the request from Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada. Her Canada student visa had been approved, and it was time to submit her passport for stamping.

“I was jumping and I was going crazy,” she says. “My daughter and my husband, and I were all crying.”

With the student visa approved, they accepted the offer from TRU, and she was admitted as a student. Afterwards, she received an acceptance from Vancouver Island University, the third of her five chosen universities to accept her. She’s happy she selected TRU.

Keshav explains that while luck helps, being prepared, getting information together and working with a knowledgeable immigration consultant help make luck happen.

Deepanjali arrived in Canada on August 5, 2019, and started her studies with TRU in September. She is now going into her third semester. She is on a sabbatical from her job in India, where she headed up the primary section of a school.

“I’ve fallen in love with this place,” she says of Kamloops. “Only time will tell how much I miss my country and my people. I will definitely consider a plan to work here if there’s a good job.”

She’s enjoying her studies while finding them challenging. Her daughter has come to visit, and her husband will in the future. She credits Keshav and his team at Colossus Migration (Immigration Consultant Surrey, Canada) for making her dream to study abroad possible when everyone else said it wasn’t.
